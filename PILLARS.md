**March 27–29, 2026 · VCU Snead Hall**

## 🏛️ A Thriving City Hall

Richmond's city government touches residents' lives every day, but navigating it can feel like a maze. Whether you're trying to file a 311 request, find the right department, or make sense of a procurement process, the information is out there — just not easy to find or act on. This pillar is about making City services work better for the people who depend on them and the staff who deliver them.

**Problem statements:**
- **Resident Service Navigation** — Help residents quickly find the right City service or next step when interacting with rva.gov and RVA311, so requests get routed correctly the first time
- **Procurement Risk & Opportunity Review** — Help City staff identify valid, compliant contracts across City, state, and federal sources with less manual PDF hunting

## 🏘️ Thriving Neighborhoods

Richmond's neighborhoods are changing fast, and residents often find out about development proposals after decisions have already been made. City planning data is public, but buried in Legistar agendas and council packets that require insider knowledge to navigate. This pillar is about giving residents earlier, clearer visibility into what's happening on their block.

**Problem statements:**
- **Neighborhood Development Discovery** — Make it easier for residents to find and understand development proposals near them so they can engage earlier in the planning process
- **Affordable Housing Compliance Monitoring** — Help City staff track whether publicly funded affordable housing stays affordable over time

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Thriving Families

For many Richmond youth, the path to a first job is unclear and unsupported — and for mothers and infants, fragmented data makes it hard to coordinate care and funding decisions across agencies. Both problems share a common thread: the right information exists somewhere, but it's scattered, inaccessible, or duplicated across organizations. This pillar is about closing those gaps for the families who need it most.

**Problem statements:**
- **Youth Employment Pathways** — Give Richmond youth one clear place to explore summer jobs, internships, and first-job guidance — and help employers understand what hiring youth requires
- **Maternal Health Data Coordination** — Reduce duplicated data analysis across agencies and nonprofits so Richmond's maternal health ecosystem can operate from shared, consistent metrics

## 💼 A Thriving Economy

Richmond has a procurement portal and a minority business enterprise program, but for a small business owner without government contracting experience, both can feel inaccessible. The same friction shows up when starting a business: multiple departments, no clear front door, and no map for what comes first. This pillar is about lowering those barriers so more Richmond entrepreneurs and minority-owned businesses can participate in the local economy.

**Problem statements:**
- **MBE Contracting Discovery** — Help minority-owned businesses identify and understand City contracting opportunities that match their capabilities, so more firms can confidently pursue public work
- **Small Business Navigation** — Help first-time and home-based entrepreneurs understand what steps to take, in what order, to start and register a business in Richmond

## 🤝 Thriving and Inclusive Communities

Richmond's immigrant and refugee communities rely on a network of trusted organizations for support — but finding those services safely, in the right language, is genuinely hard. Privacy concerns, language barriers, and fragmented information across platforms mean many residents don't seek help at all, or repeat their story to multiple providers before getting it. This pillar is about building trust and reducing friction for some of Richmond's most underserved residents.

**Problem statements:**
- **Immigrant Service Discovery** — Help residents safely find and connect to trusted community services without creating accounts or sharing identifying information — prioritizing privacy, multilingual access, and trust
- **Cross-Agency Service Navigation** — Help residents navigate housing, workforce, and reentry services without having to repeat their story across every agency and provider

## 🏗️ A Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment

When construction cones appear on a Richmond street, most residents have no reliable way to find out what the project is, who's responsible, or how long it will last. Infrastructure data exists — across ArcGIS layers, DPW program pages, and grant documents — but it's fragmented and written in technical language. This pillar is about making the physical city legible to the people who live in it.

**Problem statements:**
- **Infrastructure Project Visibility** — Help residents find and understand transportation and infrastructure projects happening in their neighborhoods, with clear status and plain-language descriptions
- **Fleet Operations & Street Service Tracking** — Improve how DPW supervisors and residents track the progress of services like snow removal and street cleaning in real time

## 🎨 A City That Tells Its Stories

Richmond's arts scene is rich and active, but event listings are scattered across galleries, nonprofits, Instagram accounts, and neighborhood email lists — making discovery a matter of luck. Meanwhile, the lived stories of long-time residents — especially from communities experiencing rapid displacement — risk being lost entirely. This pillar is about surfacing Richmond's cultural life and preserving the voices that give the city its identity.

**Problem statements:**
- **Arts & Cultural Event Discovery** — Aggregate Richmond's arts and cultural events into one discoverable place, pulling from existing sources rather than requiring new content creation
- **Resident Stories as Civic Insight** — Give residents a trusted, accessible way to share their lived experiences so community narratives can inform City decisions and be preserved
