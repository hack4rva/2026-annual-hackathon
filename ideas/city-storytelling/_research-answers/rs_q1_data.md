The StoryCorps Archive contains numerous Richmond-tagged interviews, but they come with machine-generated, unverified transcripts and a restrictive personal use license, not an open one like Creative Commons. However, several digitized oral history collections for specific Richmond neighborhoods do exist, including for Jackson Ward, Church Hill, Carver, and Oregon Hill. The Valentine Museum uses a 'Deed of Gift' model, a legal instrument that transfers ownership of materials to the museum, though specific details for comparison against Creative Commons are not publicly available. Finally, while the Richmond GeoHub offers a neighborhood boundary layer with 148 records, it is unclear without direct inspection if it distinguishes between sub-neighborhoods like Fulton and Montrose Heights.

## Platform Death Lesson

The shutdown of Detour after its acquisition highlights the significant risk of relying on a proprietary platform that can be unilaterally discontinued, leaving users and content creators without access to their work. This event underscores the critical importance of ensuring data ownership, portability, and the use of open standards for civic storytelling projects to ensure their long-term resilience and survival beyond the lifespan of a single platform.


# Rights Transfer Model Comparison

## Model Name

Deed of Gift

## Ownership Transfer

This is a standard legal instrument used by archives and museums to transfer the ownership of and legal rights to donated materials from the donor to the institution. The institution becomes the new owner of the physical and intellectual property.

## Permissions Control

Permissions are controlled by the receiving institution according to the specific terms outlined in the Deed of Gift document. This document details the conditions of the donation, including copyright and any restrictions on how the material can be used.

## Key Difference

The key difference from a Creative Commons license is that a Deed of Gift typically involves a complete transfer of ownership and copyright to an institution. In contrast, Creative Commons is a licensing model where the original creator retains their copyright but grants the public pre-approved permissions to use the work under specific conditions.
