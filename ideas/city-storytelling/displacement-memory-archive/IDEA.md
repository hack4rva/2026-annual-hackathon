# Displacement Memory Archive

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** A City That Tells Its Stories  
**Problem Statement:** Give residents a trusted, accessible way to share their lived experiences so community narratives can inform City decisions.

---

## Description

Richmond already has substantial displacement archives — they are scattered across institutions. The innovation is not building a new archive; it is **federating** existing ones into a policy-relevant tool that connects personal memories to structural evidence. Richmond’s displacement history (e.g., Jackson Ward’s I-95 corridor, Fulton Bottom) is documented across VCU, the Library of Virginia, JXN Project, The Valentine, and UofR data, but not presented as a unified, spatially grounded narrative linking oral history to policy. A federated “Richmond Displacement Memory Portal” would cross-link collections without necessarily migrating data, and could support advocacy and environmental-review use of oral history where precedent exists.

## Key Approach

- Single entry point (federated search and linking) across VCU Fulton materials, JXN Project, Library of Virginia exhibits, UofR “Renewing Inequality” and related structural data.
- Spatial narrative: georeferenced historical maps (e.g., Sanborn), pins for clips, overlays of displacement statistics, before/after views with current imagery.
- Policy pathway: package oral histories and displacement data as citable evidence for public testimony, grants, and reviews where appropriate.
- Governance: descendant and community authority over interpretation, modeled on peer cities’ advisory structures.
- Weekend-shaped prototype: ArcGIS StoryMap chapter(s) with embedded sources and citations, not wholesale data migration.
