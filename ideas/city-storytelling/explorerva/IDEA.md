# ExploreRVA

**Pillar:** Storytelling (A City That Tells Its Stories)
**Project Site:** https://explorerva.org
**Submission:** row-04

---

## Overview

ExploreRVA is a platform for discovering what's actually happening in Richmond — live events, local stories, hidden experiences — in a way that surfaces current, accurate information rather than outdated or dead links. The demo pitch is delivered as a spoken-word poem that dramatizes the problem from the user's perspective.

---

## Problem (As Told Through Spoken Word)

The team framed the problem creatively: finding real, current things to do in Richmond is frustrating. Search results surface stale pages, dead links, wrong dates, wrong addresses, and events that already happened. The city is full of life and color, but discovery tools make it feel gray.

The poem specifically calls out:
- Clicking links and finding content from "last May"
- Event pages that vanish after the event passes
- Wrong addresses and bad maps
- Stories and voices in the community that go undocumented and drift away

> *"I wanna explore RVA for real, not the echoes on my screen. Give me something that's alive, not a rerun of a dream. So much color in this town, but my search is faded gray. Too many dead ends in the way."*

---

## The Human Cost — The Man on the Corner

The second half of the poem shifts from the frustration of event discovery to something deeper: the loss of living memory. It describes an older man on the corner — a carrier of Richmond's stories — whose voice and experiences are at risk of being permanently lost because no platform captures them.

> *"There's a man on the corner with a story half told. Got a lifetime of rhythm and a voice grown low… He laughs loud, then he fades away. No one stayed long enough."*

> *"We don't lose it when the moment calls, when the memory rises and gently falls. We lose it slow, we lose it deep, when the voice goes quiet and permanently."*

This frames ExploreRVA not just as an events app, but as a platform for preserving the living culture of Richmond — the stories, people, and moments that are happening right now but won't be findable later.

---

## Demo Format

The pitch was delivered entirely as a spoken-word performance rather than a traditional slide deck or screen recording. This unconventional format was itself a demonstration of the creative, story-forward approach the team is taking with the product.
