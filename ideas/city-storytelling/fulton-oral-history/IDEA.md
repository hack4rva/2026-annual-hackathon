# Fulton Oral History Interactive Map

**Team:** Team Temporary — Caitlin, Will, Daniel, Pascal, and Monty
**Pillar:** Pillar 7 — A City That Tells Its Stories
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Richmond has become a popular destination for young people — for school at institutions like VCU and the University of Richmond, and for careers at employers like Capital One, CoStar, and the Federal Reserve. Thousands of new, young Richmonders arrive each year knowing little about the city's past or the people who built it.

The team focused on the Fulton neighborhood as a case study. Fulton was once a thriving, majority Black neighborhood — home to families, businesses, and cultural landmarks. In the 1960s, Richmond used federal funds from the 1949 Housing Act's Urban Renewal Program to clear what city planners called "blighted areas." Hundreds of families were displaced and much of the neighborhood's historic character was permanently lost. Although urban renewal promised to rebuild and revitalize Fulton, in reality it pushed out the Black residents who had built and sustained the neighborhood for generations. The effects are still visible today in the form of empty lots and an absence of historic buildings.

Many of those displaced residents are still alive, but aging — and their stories risk being lost entirely. Projects like the **Fulton Oral History Project** have worked to preserve these memories, but they remain largely inaccessible to the young people who now call Richmond home.

---

## Solution

The team built an **interactive geospatial map** that brings the Fulton Oral History Project's transcripts to life. The goal is to help young people explore Richmond's history by surfacing the stories, people, and places that shaped the city — in a format that is intuitive and engaging.

---

## How It Works — Demo Walkthrough

### Data Source
The team pulled transcripts directly from the **Fulton Oral History Project**, extracting three types of information per entry:
- Location
- Historical stories
- Direct quotes from interview subjects

### Map Interface
- The left side of the interface provides **location-based filters** — users can filter the geotags by neighborhood or area.
- Each **geotag on the map** represents a real location tied to a story or oral history entry.
- Clicking a geotag reveals:
  - Everything known about that location
  - A **direct quote** from the oral history interview associated with that spot
  - Historical context for the location

### Planned Feature (Not Yet Built)
The team plans to add a clickable link on each entry so users can go directly to the full transcript or listen to the original audio recording themselves.

## Go-to-Market and Promotion Strategy

To drive awareness and adoption among the target audience of young Richmonders:

1. **Physical QR code signage** — Posted in relevant neighborhoods so passersby can scan and access the app directly.
2. **University partnerships** — Outreach to VCU and the University of Richmond to promote the app during new student orientation, targeting residents who have just arrived in the city.

---

## Community Validation

To test demand before building, the team posted polls on their personal social media accounts. They received a **73% interest rate** from respondents who said they wanted to better understand the city they live in.

Following this, the team conducted user interviews with young adults who had expressed interest. The **top suggestion** received: make it a more interactive, guided tour format to keep users engaged during walks and runs.

### Aspirational Feature (Unlimited Time Scenario)
If the team had unlimited time, they would have added a feature allowing users to **connect their Strava accounts** so the app could generate custom history guides for the specific areas each person explores most — merging fitness habits with local history discovery.

## Expansion Plan

- Expand beyond the Fulton neighborhood by integrating additional existing datasets from across Richmond
- Build out a platform where **aging residents across the entire city** can contribute their own stories and experiences
- By demonstrating strong public interest, encourage even more residents to come forward and share their history

## Mission

> "Ultimately, our goal is to make Richmond's deep history accessible, personal, and engaging for everybody — that the stories of the people who built this city are never forgotten."
