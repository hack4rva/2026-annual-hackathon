# Planning Commission Story Feed

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** A City That Tells Its Stories  
**Problem Statement:** Give residents a trusted, accessible way to share their lived experiences so community narratives can inform City decisions.

---

## Description

Planning commission decisions are *stories about neighborhoods*: a rezoning is a narrative about what is changing on your street, not only a docket item. **Key reframe:** A subscribable “story feed” of planning decisions organized by neighborhood — RSS, email, or SMS — so residents get “what happened in my neighborhood at last night’s Planning Commission meeting?” instead of relying on dense packets and recordings. Richmond Planning Commission decisions sit in long meetings and thick agenda materials; residents who cannot attend or decode jargon often never learn what was decided. The feed combines faster time-to-understanding (draft summaries with human QA), plain language with accurate handling of legal terms, geographic context per matter, and visibility for public comment, not only motions and votes.

## Key Approach

- Ingest structured agenda and vote data (e.g., Legistar API) and produce plain-language summaries per item with mandated human review before publish.
- Organize output by neighborhood using geocoded matter addresses; offer web feed plus subscription channels.
- Link every summary to authoritative records, recordings, and public comment sources for trust and verification.
- Optional hybrid: community note-taker model alongside structured data, where research shows plain community notes can outperform raw minutes for readability and comment capture.
- Disclose AI assistance where used; keep official minutes the legal record.
