# What's On RVA

**Team:** Sri Gana (presenter), Ishika, Tejesh — VCU Graduate Students (Computer Science)
**Pillar:** Pillar 7 — A City That Tells Its Stories (Arts & Cultural Events)
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Right now, if someone in Richmond wants to find a local art or cultural event, they must check multiple platforms — Instagram, newsletters, websites, and more. The problem is not that events don't exist. The problem is that **information is fragmented** across too many places, making discovery exhausting and unreliable.

---

## Solution

**What's On RVA** is a single, unified platform where anyone can discover local arts and cultural events quickly and easily. No login is required to browse. The platform focuses on surfacing events that already exist but lack visibility, rather than replacing any existing platform.

---

## Demo Walkthrough

### Landing Page
- Users immediately see events happening in Richmond upon arrival — **no login required**
- Events are displayed in a browsable feed

### Filtering
- Filter by **date range**: this weekend, this week, or specific dates
- Filter by **event type** (e.g., theater, music, visual art)
- Filter by **price**: free or paid events
- Filters help users plan their night instead of browsing randomly

### Personalized Experience
- Users who want curated or personalized recommendations can optionally **register for an account**
- Selecting event preferences and neighborhood helps tailor results

### Neighborhood Stories Section
- A dedicated section allows users to explore **specific Richmond neighborhoods** — Jackson Ward, Carver, Scott's Addition, and others
- Each neighborhood includes stories connected to its culture, history, and community

### Map View
- A full **map view** shows where events and stories are located across Richmond
- Users can explore based on physical location
- Clicking any location on the map **opens the associated story or event directly**

### AI Chat Assistant
- Users can ask simple, conversational questions such as:
  - *"What can I do tonight?"*
  - *"Plan my night for me"*
- The system responds with **suggestions based on available events**, eliminating the need to manually search

---

## Key Insight

> "This enrichment doesn't lack any events — it lacks visibility. What's On RVA makes it easier to discover, explore, and plan experiences in one place."

---

## What It Is Not

The team is explicit: What's On RVA is **not replacing existing platforms** like Instagram or event newsletters. Its primary function is to aggregate and surface what already exists, making the full picture of Richmond's arts scene discoverable from one place.
