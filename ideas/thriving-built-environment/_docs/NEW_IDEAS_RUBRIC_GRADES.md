## Idea 12: GRTC–Construction Conflict Detector

| Category | Score | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Impact | 4 | Addresses a critical compound harm: construction → blocked stops → missed pickups → CARE service suspension for disabled riders. |
| Feasibility | 4 | GRTC publishes GTFS (stop locations); GeoHub has project geometries; spatial join is standard GIS. CARE data requires GRTC partnership. |
| User Value | 5 | Prevents stranding of transit-dependent residents; prevents CARE no-show penalties that compound into 14-day suspensions. |
| Execution | 4 | Nightly spatial join of two public datasets is hackathon-achievable; alert delivery adds modest complexity. |
| Innovation | 5 | No city has cross-referenced construction zones with paratransit routing to protect vulnerable riders. Genuinely novel. |
| Equity | 5 | Explicitly protects disabled, elderly, and transit-dependent residents — the populations most harmed by infrastructure disruptions. |

## Idea 5: ADA Detour Digital Publisher

| Category | Score | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Impact | 4 | Addresses a critical equity gap for a smaller but severely affected population — wheelchair users, visually impaired, paratransit riders. |
| Feasibility | 4 | Detour data exists (required in Work-in-Street Permits); digitization pipeline is achievable. GRTC CARE integration requires partnership. |
| User Value | 5 | Transforms an absolute barrier (arriving at a closed sidewalk with no alternative) into a planned detour. Prevents CARE no-show penalties. |
| Execution | 3 | Requires understanding permit data format and creating accessible turn-by-turn output — more complex than a standard dashboard. |
| Innovation | 5 | No city has published digital ADA detour routes from construction permits. Genuinely novel approach to construction accessibility. |
| Equity | 5 | Core purpose is equity for the most physically vulnerable residents. Prevents compounding harms (closures → missed pickups → service suspensions). |
