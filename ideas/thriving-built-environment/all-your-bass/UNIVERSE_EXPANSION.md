# Beyond the Plow: Scaling Richmond's "Stay Informed" into a Regional Civic Infrastructure Standard

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The "Stay Informed" (all-your-bass) prototype addresses a critical friction point in Richmond's urban experience: the information asymmetry between City Hall's operational reality and the resident's curb-side expectations. While the hackathon demo successfully visualized the concept of transparency, its long-term viability depends on shifting from a "tracker" (which requires expensive, real-time GPS hardware) to a "service contract" (which focuses on schedule reliability and resident compliance). 

To move beyond a static prototype, the project must pivot toward a Regional Hybrid Model. By housing the data-sharing infrastructure within PlanRVA and the resident-facing engagement layer within a civic nonprofit, the City of Richmond can mitigate procurement risks while tapping into state-level innovation funds like the VDOT Innovation and Technology Transportation Fund (ITTF) [1] and federal demonstration grants like the USDOT SMART program [2]. This expansion transforms a local snow-plow map into a regional "Civic Reliability Engine" applicable to leaf collection, street sweeping, and emergency response across Central Virginia.

## 2. EXPANDED OWNERSHIP RECOMMENDATION

**Recommended Model: Regional Hybrid Model**

The most viable path forward is a distributed ownership model that leverages the strengths of different institutional layers:

* **What the City Should Own (Data Generation & Operations):** The City of Richmond's Department of Public Works (DPW) should own the raw operational data and the internal staff portal. Their responsibility is strictly to provide accurate status updates (whether via GPS telemetry or manual supervisor inputs) and manage the actual fleet operations.
* **What a Regional Body Should Own (Data Standardization & Hosting):** PlanRVA, the federally designated Metropolitan Planning Organization (MPO) for the Richmond region [3], should own the data standard and API infrastructure. PlanRVA is uniquely positioned to analyze, plan, and consult across the region's nine localities [4].
* **What a Nonprofit Should Own (Resident Experience & UI):** A civic tech nonprofit (e.g., Code for America brigade or local civic group) should own the public-facing resident portal. This ensures the interface remains user-centric, agile, and free from municipal procurement bottlenecks.
* **What Should NOT be City-Owned:** The public-facing application layer. City-owned public tech often suffers from deferred maintenance and poor UX. Offloading this to a nonprofit partner ensures continuous improvement and community trust.

## 6. SCALE SHIFT ANALYSIS

Framing this idea solely as a Richmond project limits its potential and funding. Snow, storms, and infrastructure needs do not stop at the city line. 

If the frame becomes regional (managed by PlanRVA), the project transforms from a local symptom-fix into a systemic infrastructure asset. A regional approach allows neighboring counties (Henrico, Chesterfield) to plug into the same data standard, creating a unified experience for residents who commute across municipal boundaries. Furthermore, a regional scope makes the project highly competitive for federal USDOT SMART grants [2], which prioritize scalable, multi-jurisdictional demonstration projects over isolated local apps.

## 7. RISK AND CONSTRAINT ANALYSIS

* **The "Synthetic Data" Risk:** The current prototype relies on mock data. If deployed without real DPW feeds, it creates a severe trust deficit and potential liability if residents make safety decisions based on fictional plow locations.
* **Procurement Hurdles:** Waiting for a full fleet GPS/AVL hardware procurement cycle could delay the project by years.
* **Labor Relations:** DPW staff may view real-time tracking as a "Big Brother" surveillance tool rather than an operational aid.
* **Equity and Accountability:** Without a 311-integrated feedback loop to report missed services, the tool risks becoming a one-way broadcast that ignores underserved neighborhoods.
