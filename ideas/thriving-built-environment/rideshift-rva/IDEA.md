# RideShift RVA

**Team:** The Supply and Command Line
**Pillars Addressed:** Thriving & Sustainable Built Environment + Thriving Economy
**Website:** rideshiftrva.com
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Too many Richmond commutes start and end in a single car. Traffic builds, emissions rise, and roads — already under constant construction — absorb the impact. Richmond has committed to the shift to net zero emissions under the **RVA Green 2050 plan**, and the infrastructure to support that shift already exists. What's missing is the incentive for individuals to actually change their habits.

---

## Solution

**RideShift RVA** incentivizes Richmonders to rethink their transportation habits and mix up their commute by offering **rewards from local businesses**. The approach simultaneously:
- Gets cars off the road
- Boosts Richmond's local economy
- Builds community through shared sustainability goals

---

## Demo Walkthrough

### Sign-Up Flow
1. Enter your email address
2. Receive a **magic link** via email
3. Click the link to sign in — no password required

### Goal Setting
- After signing in, users set personal transportation goals
- Example from demo: *"I like to bike — I'll add a scooter day as well"*
- Goals are self-defined and flexible

### Weekly Check-In
- At the end of each week, users receive an **email asking about their goal**
- Whether or not they met the goal, they can **select a reward**
- The philosophy: *"The aim is progress, not perfection"*

### Barrier Reporting
- Users can also **input barriers to transit** they experienced during the week
- This data helps the city understand what's actually preventing people from using multimodal transit

## On-Site Resources

While on the RideShift RVA site, users can access a full library of transit resources:
- **GRTC maps and schedules**
- **Bike infrastructure and advocacy** information
- **E-scooter operators** in Richmond
- **Carpool ride finder tool**
- **Pedestrian safety information**

---

## Richmond Connects Integration

Users can view **active and planned transportation projects** on the Richmond Connects map directly from the site. From there, they can click through to **provide input directly to the City of Richmond** — turning passive riders into engaged civic participants.

This directly aligns with the **Thriving & Sustainable Built Environment** pillar by:
- Shifting toward net zero greenhouse gas emissions through improved multimodal transportation
- Reimagining safer streets
- Increasing transportation project visibility and discoverability

---

## Local Business Partnerships — Thriving Economy Alignment

In just **36 hours**, the following Richmond businesses committed to offering deals through RideShift RVA:
1. **Common House**
2. **Tito's Taqueria**
3. **Bolton Hot Natives**
4. **Rushing Blooms**
5. **West Broad Studios**

All are local, small, and minority-owned businesses. By driving traffic to these businesses as rewards, RideShift RVA serves as a direct economic development tool for Richmond's independent business community.
