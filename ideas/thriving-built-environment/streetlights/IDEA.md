## Problem Statement

> "Imagine you're in a rush and suddenly your usual route is blocked by construction. You're frustrated and you ask yourself — why is this happening? Why now?"

Right now, Richmond residents have no easy way to see what city projects are happening on their street or how long they'll last. This creates confusion, unexpected delays, and a disconnect between residents and their city's infrastructure activity.

---

## Solution

**StreetLights** is a website that translates complex government data into **clear, plain-English information** for ordinary residents. Instead of technical jargon, every project is explained in language that anyone can understand. Data is pulled live from **Richmond GeoHub**.

---

## Demo Walkthrough

### Step 1 — Enter Your Address
- The user begins by entering their address
- The site instantly displays a list of **nearby city projects** within a radius the user chooses
- The radius is configurable, giving residents control over how wide a view they want

### Step 2 — Interactive Map
- An **interactive map** appears alongside the list
- Users can zoom in and out to easily locate projects
- Each map marker represents a **real, active city project**

### Step 3 — Project Details
Clicking any map marker reveals:
- **What the project is** (in plain English)
- **When it will be completed**
- **Project manager contact information** — so residents can actually reach someone

### Step 4 — Filtering by Project Type
- Users can filter projects by **project type** (e.g., construction, road work, utility)
- Each project is **color-coded by status**:
  - In construction
  - In design
  - In planning
- A **legend** is displayed in the bottom-left corner of the map for reference

---

## Additional Features

### Desktop and Mobile
- StreetLights is fully accessible on both **desktop and mobile**, making it accessible to residents regardless of device

### SMS Notifications
- Residents can **sign up for SMS notifications** to stay updated on nearby projects without ever opening the website
- Notifications are delivered directly to their phone

### Community Issue Reporting
- If users spot problems in real life — potholes, broken infrastructure, hazards — they can **report them directly** from the site
- Other users can **upvote the same issue**, creating a community prioritization system
- The most urgently upvoted problems receive prioritized attention from the platform

---

## Planned Future Features

### Route Planning with Construction Awareness
- The team plans to add **destination input** so users can see the shortest route from their current location to a destination **while avoiding construction zones**
- This would turn StreetLights from an information tool into an active navigation aid
