# Beyond the Chatbot: Scaling Hey804 as Richmond's Multi-Channel Civic Triage Layer

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Hey804 represents a critical architectural shift in civic AI: moving from "generative conversation" (which failed in New York City) to "deterministic triage." By using LLMs only for classification and grounding answers in verified city data, Hey804 solves the hallucination problem that has plagued municipal AI. However, its current web-only implementation risks deepening the digital divide, as it excludes the approximately 12% of Richmonders who are cellular-only. 

To fulfill Mayor Avula's "Thriving City Hall" pillar, Hey804 must evolve from a Richmond-specific web widget into a regional, multi-channel infrastructure. The optimal path is a Hybrid Ownership Model: the City of Richmond maintains the "Knowledge Base" (the source of truth), while a regional entity like PlanRVA or a nonprofit partner manages the multi-channel delivery (SMS/Voice) and scales the tool to surrounding jurisdictions. This approach unlocks federal Digital Equity Act funding and bypasses the procurement bottlenecks of traditional city IT projects.

## 2. EXPANDED OWNERSHIP RECOMMENDATION

**Recommended Model: Hybrid (City-Data / Regional-Nonprofit Managed)**

* **What the City Should Own:** The **Knowledge Base (Source of Truth)**. City departments must remain the sole authorities on their own rules, forms, and URLs. The City should also own the API Gateway that allows the bot to talk to RVA311.
* **What Partners Should Own:** The **User Interface and Multi-Channel Delivery**. A regional body (PlanRVA) or a civic nonprofit (e.g., a local Code for America brigade) should manage the frontend, SMS integrations, and user testing. This allows for faster iteration than City Hall's internal IT cycles.
* **What Should NOT be City-Owned:** The **LLM Infrastructure**. Relying on third-party hosted models (Claude/Azure) is more cost-effective than the City attempting to host private instances, provided no Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is transmitted.

## 5. EXISTING EFFORTS / PRIOR ART

* **NYC MyCity AI Chatbot (New York City):** 
 * *What was attempted:* An AI chatbot for small business owners.
 * *Outcome:* **Failure**. The bot repeatedly served up false and damaging information, encouraging users to break the law, and was shut down [16] [17].
 * *Lesson:* Never let the AI "generate" policy; only "classify" and "link."
* **Sunny Chatbot (Denver, CO):** 
 * *What was attempted:* A municipal chatbot to handle resident inquiries.
 * *Outcome:* **Success**. Handled high volumes of questions at a significantly reduced cost per interaction compared to human agents.
 * *Lesson:* High-volume FAQs are the best ROI for municipal AI.
* **Decatur Direct (Decatur, GA):** 
 * *What was attempted:* An AI chatbot powered by Ordinal Connect.
 * *Outcome:* **Success**. The assistant is trained strictly on the City's approved documents and public materials [18].
 * *Lesson:* Citation-based answers grounded in official documents are the only way to maintain trust.

## 6. SCALE SHIFT ANALYSIS

* **The "Symptom" Fix:** Framed as a Richmond-only web widget, Hey804 is just a "better search bar" for people who already know how to find `rva.gov`. It fails to address the digital divide for those without internet access.
* **The "System" Fix:** Framed as a **Regional Triage Layer**, Hey804 becomes the "Front Door" for the entire Richmond Metro area.
 * *If a nonprofit owns the layer:* It can integrate non-governmental services (food banks, shelters) that the City cannot legally "endorse" on its official homepage.
 * *If the frame is Statewide:* It could serve as a model for the Virginia Department of Housing and Community Development (DHCD) to provide a "One Front Door" for state-level benefits, aligning with the state's digital opportunity goals [19].

## 7. RISK AND CONSTRAINT ANALYSIS

* **Data Staleness (Critical):** If the SQLite database isn't synced with the CMS, the bot will provide "dead links," destroying resident trust.
* **Procurement (High):** Integrating with the RVA311 backend may require a formal contract amendment, which can take months.
* **The "Digital Divide" Paradox:** By making the website easier to use, the City may inadvertently ignore the need for better physical/phone-based services for those offline.
* **VITA Compliance:** Per the 2024 State Policy, any AI system developed, implemented, or procured must be entered into a registry maintained by VITA [13]. Failure to do so could block state funding.
