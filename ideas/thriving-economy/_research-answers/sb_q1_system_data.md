# Executive Summary

To start a business in Richmond, Virginia, entrepreneurs must follow a specific sequence of registrations. The process typically begins at the state level by forming the business entity with the Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC), which can sometimes be approved the same day if filed online. This is followed by obtaining a federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS, which is generally free. Next, the business must register with Virginia Tax for relevant state taxes. Only then can the local Richmond-specific steps be taken, starting with obtaining a Certificate of Zoning Compliance (CZC) through the city's Online Permit Portal; a home occupation CZC costs $50 and typically takes about 10 business days. With the CZC approved, the final major step is applying for the City of Richmond Business, Professional and Occupational License (BPOL) within 30 days of opening. BPOL costs vary: businesses with gross receipts under $250,000 pay a flat fee ($0 or $30), while those above pay a rate based on business type (e.g., 0.20% for retail, 0.58% for professional services). A significant finding is the lack of modern data access for city-level processes; the RVA Business Portal, even after its January 2025 modernization, does not offer any public API or webhook capabilities for accessing BPOL tax rate schedules or other data. In contrast, the Virginia SCC does provide extensive data access through the Virginia Open Data Portal, offering both bulk downloads (CSV/XLSX) and API access for datasets including corporations, LLCs, officers, and amendments.

# Business Registration Sequence

The complete, ordered sequence for registering a new business in Richmond, Virginia is as follows:
1.  **Form or Register the Business Entity:** Register the business with the Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC). This includes registering any fictitious or trade names. This state-level step must precede local steps.
2.  **Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN):** Secure an EIN from the IRS, which is necessary for state tax registration and the city business license application.
3.  **Register with Virginia Tax:** Register the business for all applicable state taxes (e.g., sales and use, withholding) to receive state account numbers and necessary certificates.
4.  **Obtain a Certificate of Zoning Compliance (CZC):** Apply for and receive an approved CZC from the Richmond Department of Planning and Development Review through the Online Permit Portal. This is a mandatory prerequisite for the city business license.
5.  **Apply for the City of Richmond Business License (BPOL):** Within 30 days of opening, apply for the Business, Professional and Occupational License (BPOL) via the RVA Business Portal.
6.  **Obtain Business-Type-Specific Permits:** Secure any additional permits required for the specific industry, such as those from the Health Department, Virginia ABC for alcohol, or DPOR for contractors, as listed on the city's checklist.

## Step Name

Apply for City of Richmond Business, Professional and Occupational License (BPOL)

## Agency

City of Richmond, Department of Finance

## Cost

Cost is based on gross receipts: $0 for receipts ≤ $5,000; $30 for receipts from $5,001 to $250,000. For receipts ≥ $250,000, a tax rate is applied (e.g., $0.20/$100 for Retail, $0.58/$100 for Professional Service). Additional flat fees apply for certain business types (e.g., $75 for Beer & Wine, $300 for Peddlers). The gross receipts threshold for rate-based tax increases to $500,000 on Jan 1, 2026.

## Timeline

New businesses must obtain a license within 30 days of opening. Licenses expire on December 31 and must be renewed by March 1 annually. Late penalties are 10% or $10 (whichever is greater), with 10% annual interest.

## Description

An annual license required for all businesses operating within the City of Richmond. Applications are submitted through the RVA Business Portal. An approved CZC is required before applying.

## Cost

Varies depending on the specific permit or license.

## Timeline

Varies depending on the specific permit or license.

## Description

Depending on the nature of the business, additional permits may be necessary. The city's checklist includes examples such as Health Department permits for food service, ABC licenses for alcohol, DPOR licenses for contractors, and special permits for peddlers or dance halls.

# Business License Checklist Format

The City of Richmond maintains its Business License Application Checklist solely as a PDF document. Research of the city's public-facing websites and document repositories, including rva.gov, found no evidence of a structured, machine-readable version of this checklist, such as a JSON, CSV, or XML file. The official document, titled "25' New Business License Checklist & Application.pdf," is a static file designed for human reading and manual completion, not for automated data extraction or integration into software systems.

# Canonical Business Requirements List

No, a single, canonical, machine-readable list that maps Richmond, VA business types to all their required permits, licenses, and tax obligations does not exist. The information is decentralized and must be compiled by consulting multiple, disparate sources. For example, BPOL tax rates and some specific documentation needs (e.g., for contractors or restaurants) are found on the Finance department's BPOL webpage. A different set of potential requirements, such as Health Department permits, ABC licenses, or DPOR contractor licenses, are listed as if-applicable items within the static Business License Checklist PDF. Furthermore, critical zoning requirements, like obtaining a Certificate of Zoning Compliance (CZC), are detailed on the separate Zoning Administration and Permits & Inspections webpages. This fragmentation requires business owners to manually navigate and synthesize information from these various city resources to ensure full compliance.

# Certificate Of Zoning Compliance Czc

## Application Process

All applications for a Certificate of Zoning Compliance (CZC) must be submitted through the City of Richmond's Online Permit Portal (OPP). This certificate is a prerequisite and must be obtained before applying for a City business license. It serves as clearance for compliance with zoning and building code regulations for either a home occupation or a commercial building.

## Cost

For a home-based business (Home Occupation), the application fee is $50.00. For commercial properties, the fees are determined by the Zoning Office Fees schedule, which is referenced on the Zoning Administration's webpage.

## Processing Timeline

After the application fee has been paid, the approved permit is typically issued within 10 business days, assuming no unforeseen circumstances. The approved permit is sent electronically via email or by mail, and either copy can be used to apply for the business license.

## Validity

The Certificate of Zoning Compliance is typically a one-time process for a business at a specific location. It does not have a recurring renewal requirement like the annual business license.

## Reapplication Triggers

While the CZC is generally a one-time process, a new application would be required if the business changes its physical location, as the certificate provides clearance for a specific 'Home Occupation or Commercial Building'. The provided documents do not explicitly list other triggers like a change in ownership or business name, but a change in location necessitates a new zoning compliance check.


# State Corporation Commission Scc Registration

Registering a business entity is a foundational step that must be completed with the Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC). The SCC recommends filing online, noting that approvals can be granted more quickly, sometimes on the same day. This registration is mandatory for both new in-state entities and out-of-state businesses intending to transact business in Virginia. Additionally, if the business plans to operate under an assumed or fictitious trade name, that name must also be registered with the SCC for a fee. Proof of this registration must accompany the business license application submission to the City of Richmond.

# Federal And State Tax Registration

After forming the business entity, the next steps involve federal and state tax registration. First, a business must obtain a Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS, which is generally available for free. This EIN is required for state tax registration. Following that, the business must register with the Virginia Department of Taxation for all applicable tax types, such as sales and use tax or employee withholding tax. This can be done through the Virginia Tax online portal. Upon successful registration, the business will receive a Virginia Tax account number for each tax type and, if applicable, a sales tax certificate of registration.
