# CareerBridge

**Presenter:** Jake Yazbek
**Team:** Team River City Hackers
**Pillar:** Thriving Economy
**Platform:** Web and mobile web application
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Many Richmond residents feel stuck in their careers — unable to envision a new path because they lack direction, information, or support. The barriers include:
- Not knowing what careers exist or what they pay
- Not knowing what training is required to get there
- Not understanding what specific barriers have prevented them from upskilling in the past (childcare, transportation, cost, time)

---

## Solution

**CareerBridge** is a web and mobile web application for Richmonders to find their next career and build the life they want. It combines:
- A **quiz-based career matching experience**
- **Richmond-specific job and career data**
- **AI-powered matching and Q&A**
- Personalized **barrier identification and resource matching**
- **Colorblind-accessible design**

---

## Demo Walkthrough

### Step 1 — The Quiz
- The user goes through a series of questions designed to identify potential career paths that would be a great fit
- Questions are rapid and focused on the user's situation, preferences, and background

### Step 2 — AI-Powered Matching
- Answers are matched against a **database of Richmond-specific jobs**
- AI analyzes the responses to suggest which careers would be the best fit

### Step 3 — The Card Swipe Interface
- Results are displayed as a **stack of career cards**
- Swipe left = No
- Swipe right = Yes
- Or click the buttons below the card
- Example: The user swipes right on "Physical Therapist"

### Step 4 — Review Matches
- At any point the user can tap "See Matches" to review all the careers they've said yes to
- They can continue swiping or go straight to detailed career exploration

### Step 5 — Account Creation (Optional)
- Users can save their progress at any time by creating a **free account**
- No account is required to start using the app

### Step 6 — Colorblind Mode
- From the profile page, users can toggle **colorblind mode**
- When enabled: green/red UI elements switch to **blue/orange**
- Card swipe indicators also change from green/red to **orange/blue**

### Step 7 — Career Detail View
Selecting a career (e.g., "Physical Therapist Assistant") opens a full career profile showing:

#### Fit Analysis
- How this career matches based on the user's quiz answers

#### Resources Available
- Personalized based on the user's situation
- Example: If the user has children, a **Child Care Subsidy Program** is surfaced as a relevant resource
- Example resources linked:
  - **Community College program at J. Sargent Reynolds** — with a direct link to explore
  - **Virginia Nursing and Allied Health Scholarships** — potential funding source

#### Growth Data
- **22% projected growth** in this career in Virginia over the next 10 years

### Step 8 — AI Follow-Up Q&A
- Users can ask follow-up questions directly about the career
- Example question: *"How does retail work for this job? Is my experience something I can carry over?"*
- AI responds: *"Yes — tech in retail is huge"* — and explains how prior experience is relevant
- Users can ask as many follow-up questions as needed to build confidence in the career path

## Data Integration

- Already integrated with **publicly accessible career and job data**
- Further integration with **government-specific data** is planned to make results even more precise and locally relevant

## Design Philosophy

> "What started as an overlooked or dismissed skilled trades career becomes a fun, interactive environment for a person to evaluate and think — hey, this career is a great fit for me and a great fit for my family."

CareerBridge is explicitly designed to be **lightweight** — a tool that makes career change decisions easier and more accessible for Richmond residents at any stage of life.
