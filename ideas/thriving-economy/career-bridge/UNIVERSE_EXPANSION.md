# Scaling CareerBridge: A Regional Strategy for Richmond's Workforce Navigation

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CareerBridge represents a critical "navigation layer" currently missing from the Richmond ecosystem. While the hackathon prototype was framed under the Thriving Economy pillar, research indicates its true value lies in Economic Mobility and Youth Employment Pathways. The tool should not exist as a standalone city app, but as a regional interface that bridges the gap between Richmond's 675,240-person workforce [1] and the fragmented state and federal resource landscape. 

The optimal path forward is a Hybrid-Regional Model. The City of Richmond, via the Office of Community Wealth Building (OCWB), should serve as the initial pilot site and data contributor [2], while PlanRVA or the Capital Region Workforce Partnership provides the regional backbone [3] [4]. This ensures the tool captures the net 182 new residents per week entering the metro area [1] and taps into state-level funding like G3 and FastForward that a city-only tool would struggle to sustain [5] [6].

## 2. EXPANDED OWNERSHIP RECOMMENDATION

**Recommended Model: Hybrid Regional-Nonprofit Lead**

* **What the City Should Own (Data Contribution Layer):** The City, specifically the Office of Community Wealth Building, should provide the localized "Resource Directory" of city-specific training programs and serve as the physical pilot location via its Career Stations [2].
* **What a Regional Body Should Own (Infrastructure and Governance):** PlanRVA or the Capital Region Workforce Partnership should manage the employer relationships across the nine-locality region and ensure WIOA compliance [3] [4].
* **What a Nonprofit Should Own (User Experience and Outreach):** A nonprofit like United Way or Goodwill should own the user-facing layer, integrating the tool into existing financial stability programs like THRIVE [7] [8].
* **What should NOT be City-Owned (Software Codebase):** To avoid procurement and maintenance death spirals, the software should be managed as an open-source project or by a regional tech-nonprofit partner.

## 5. EXISTING EFFORTS / PRIOR ART

* **Virginia Career Works Dashboard:** A state-level tool providing labor market data and high-demand occupation lists [26]. *Lesson:* It has the data but lacks the swipe-based UX and barrier-matching that CareerBridge offers. CareerBridge should pull from this via API rather than recreating it.
* **NYC Workforce1 Sector Centers:** Physical centers focused on specific industries that achieved significantly higher earnings for participants [27]. *Lesson:* Digital tools must lead to sector-specific training tied to employer demand to see real wage gains.
* **Lightcast Career Coach:** A commercial product used by community colleges to guide jobseekers [28]. *Lesson:* It is a generic tool; CareerBridge's Barrier Matching (childcare/transit) is its unique competitive advantage for the local population.

## 6. SCALE SHIFT ANALYSIS

Framing CareerBridge solely as a Richmond project is too small because the regional workforce operates across municipal lines. The Richmond MSA has a workforce of over 675,000 people [1]. A city-only tool ignores the jobs in neighboring counties. Moving to a PlanRVA frame allows the tool to access GO Virginia funds, which require regional collaboration [9]. If a nonprofit owns the resident-facing layer while the city provides data, the tool can follow the user even if they move out of the city, maintaining a longitudinal record of their career growth.

## 7. RISK AND CONSTRAINT ANALYSIS

* **Data Decay:** If the Richmond-specific data isn't automated via API (e.g., from Virginia Works), the app will quickly become obsolete.
* **Trust & Legitimacy:** Residents may be skeptical of an AI-matching tool if it doesn't lead to tangible outcomes like a guaranteed interview or specific scholarship.
* **Maintenance Burden:** A City-owned app risks a lack of updates and security patches due to IT backlogs.
* **Digital Divide:** A mobile-web app still requires data/Wi-Fi. Integration with physical Career Stations [2] is mandatory to reach the most vulnerable populations.
