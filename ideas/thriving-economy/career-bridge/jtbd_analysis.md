# Output Report

## Functional Job To Be Done

When I’m deciding to open my first small business location in the City of Richmond, I (first-time entrepreneur) want to see a single, step-by-step checklist that confirms my location is allowed (zoning), what permits I need, and how to get my business license, so I can legally open with confidence and a predictable timeline.

## Functional Job Current Workaround

Piecing together steps across the Online Permit Portal, calling the Permit Center, asking peers, and visiting multiple city and state sites (SCC, VA Tax, OMBD, SBSD) to infer the sequence.

## Functional Job Core Pain

Unclear ordering and dependencies (e.g., Certificate of Zoning Compliance before BPOL), multiple logins/portals, and long review cycles if a step is missed.

## Emotional Job To Be Done

When I’m investing savings and signing a lease, I (aspiring entrepreneur from Richmond) want assurance from official sources that I’m on the right track and won’t be surprised by last-minute requirements, so I can feel confident committing funds and timelines.

## Emotional Job Current Workaround

Calling city staff for verbal guidance, relying on community word-of-mouth, and over-researching state/federal pages to cross-check.

## Emotional Job Core Pain

Anxiety from conflicting or incomplete guidance, fear of penalties or delays, and uncertainty about special rules (e.g., restaurants, contractors).

## Systems Job To Be Done

When my startup needs to navigate city permitting, licensing, and local incentives, I (entrepreneur or counselor/SBDC advisor) want a coordinated pathway that surfaces required steps, eligibility for incentives, and equity-focused resources in one flow, so I can minimize rework and connect to capital and support programs.

## Systems Job Current Workaround

Manually tracking prerequisites across RDA/EDA incentives, Permit Center, Finance (BPOL/BPP), OMBD portals, and SBDC/accelerators.

## Systems Job Core Pain

Fragmented systems (EnerGov OPP, RVA Business Portal, B2GNow Supplier Diversity), duplicative data entry, and missed opportunities (e.g., Enterprise Zone/CARE, ESB/MBE certification).

## Data Questions

* What is the canonical step order and machine-readable rules for Richmond (e.g., CZC before BPOL, when restaurants need Health Permit, contractor thresholds), including exceptions and timelines by permit type?
* Can we access API/exports for EnerGov Online Permit Portal (application status, milestones, fees) and the RVA Business Portal (license application/renewal status)?
* What datasets identify zoning by parcel/use and link to CZC requirements, including any historic overlays or special districts affecting small retail/food businesses?
* What program eligibility data (Enterprise Zone/CARE, tech BPOL waivers) can be integrated to pre-screen users and estimate benefits?

## User Questions

* Which first-time entrepreneur segments are most active in Richmond (e.g., food trucks, salons/barbers, home-based, retail, trades), and what are their top 3 pain points by segment?
* What languages and accessibility needs are most common among new entrepreneurs, and how do we provide ADA-compliant, multilingual guidance?
* What trust anchors do Richmond entrepreneurs rely on (e.g., SBDC, OMBD, Startup Virginia, Lighthouse, libraries, neighborhood orgs), and where should onboarding live?

## Integration Questions

* Can career-bridge embed single sign-on or deep links into EnerGov OPP, RVA Business Portal (BPP/BPOL), SBSD BOS, and OMBD B2GNow, to reduce re-entry of data?
* How will the tool surface appointments or warm handoffs to TechDesk (permit consults), SBDC counseling, OMBD workshops, and Health Department pre-opening checks?
* What governance and maintenance model ensures content stays current with city code changes, fee schedules, and portal updates?

## Equity Questions

* How will the tool proactively route users to ESB/MBE/DBE certification, SWaM, and local supplier diversity opportunities—and measure uptake among underrepresented founders?
* How will we support entrepreneurs in lower-wealth neighborhoods with limited broadband/devices (mobile-first, SMS, library kiosks, paper handouts with QR)?
* What language, reading-level, and UX supports reduce drop-off for first-gen entrepreneurs unfamiliar with government processes?

## Prior Art Questions

* How should we complement, not duplicate, Virginia Business One Stop, the RVA Business Portal, OMBD Supplier Diversity portal, and SBDC resources—what gaps remain (e.g., personalized sequencing, status aggregation, eligibility screening)?
* Which navigation patterns from comparable cities’ business portals (and Richmond’s existing OPP/RVA portals) have highest completion and lowest error rates that we can emulate?
