### Judging note (six scoring categories, 1–5)

- **Impact:** Low relative to **Thriving Economy** MAP outcomes (MBE participation, contracting access, business formation sequencing). High *potential* impact could be argued only if the project were reframed under **Thriving Families / Youth Employment Pathways**.  
- **Feasibility:** Reasonable for a prototype; long-term feasibility depends on **data contracts**, **resource curation**, and **institutional partnerships** — none demonstrated end-to-end for City or youth programs.  
- **User Value:** **May score well** — clear user (resident exploring careers), tangible features (quiz, swipe, resources, AI Q&A). Value is real for a **career** job, not for **Economy PS1/PS2**.  
- **Execution:** **May score well** — working flow, colorblind mode, optional accounts, coherent demo narrative.  
- **Innovation:** Moderate to strong for **engagement mechanics** (cards, barriers); not innovative for Economy pillar *as defined*.  
- **Equity & Inclusion:** Colorblind accessibility is a concrete plus; broader equity (language, digital divide, youth-specific barriers) not fully evidenced in transcript.

## 2. What the Demo Does Well

- **Barrier identification + resource matching:** Surfaces situation-specific help (e.g., childcare subsidy when the user has children) and training/scholarship links — a credible pattern for guided social service / education navigation.  
- **Accessibility:** Explicit **colorblind mode** (green/red → blue/orange) including swipe affordances — shows attention to visual accessibility beyond default palettes.  
- **Quiz UX:** Fast, structured intake to reduce paralysis for people who feel “stuck” in their careers.  
- **AI follow-up Q&A:** Lets users ask career-specific questions (e.g., retail experience transfer) and get conversational support alongside structured data.  
- **Engagement:** Card stack / swipe (and button fallback) makes **exploration** feel lightweight and reversible.  
- **Optional accounts:** Progress save without forcing signup lowers early friction.  

---

## 3. Fundamentally Missed

The team submitted under **Thriving Economy**, but CareerBridge is **not** about **MBE contracting discovery** or **small business navigation**. It does not help minority-owned firms find or understand **City solicitations**, nor does it sequence **business registration, licensing, or permitting** for new entrepreneurs.

The core miss is **pillar / problem-statement fit**: the demo addresses **career exploration and job pathways** for **job seekers**, which aligns far more naturally with **Thriving Families — Youth Employment Pathways** than with either Economy problem statement. Until that mismatch is acknowledged, **Impact** against the submitted pillar remains fundamentally weak regardless of polish.

## 5. Next Steps

1. **Treat CareerBridge as a Families / workforce product for judging or follow-up** — score **Impact** and **User Value** against **Youth Employment Pathways** (or equivalent JTBDs) rather than forcing fit to Economy PS1/PS2.
