# Richmond Business Launch Wizard

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** A Thriving Economy  
**Problem Statement:** Help first-time entrepreneurs understand what steps to take, in what order, to start and register a business in Richmond.

---

## Description

**Core assumption challenged:** First-time entrepreneurs need a comprehensive portal; the analysis argues they need a **sequenced, address-aware decision engine** — not generic “how to start a business” pages that mostly link out to agencies. First-time Richmond entrepreneurs cannot determine permits, registrations, order of operations, cost, and duration without multiple agency touches and late surprises. Strongest evidence in the research is from cities with true wizards (high self-service rates and staff time savings), versus static guides that redirect without transactional depth. The analysis also highlights **time-to-open** estimates from historical processing patterns as a differentiator worth creating.

## Key Approach

- Steps: business description + planned address → zoning eligibility signal → sequenced permits/registrations → itemized fees → estimated timeline → checklist with links to applications → printable or emailable action plan.
- Uses address-first zoning where GeoHub (or similar) supports it; otherwise narrow curated paths for top business types.
- Targets entrepreneurs and staff who field repetitive intake questions; optional analytics on demand by business type and geography for economic development.
- Weekend MVP shaped as a narrow, accurate slice (few business types) rather than broad shallow coverage.
- Avoid generative AI for regulatory content; use structured rules and clear disclaimers.
