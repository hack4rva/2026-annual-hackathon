# Richmond Contract Navigator

**Presenter:** Mia Wan
**Team:** Team Silverlight
**Pillar:** Thriving Economy — Pillar 4 (Minority & First-Time Business Contracting Access)
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

The problem is not that Richmond's city contracting opportunities are hidden. The information is public. The real barrier is:
- **Legibility** — contract listings use jargon that insiders understand but newcomers don't
- **Complexity** — terms like "IFB" (Invitation for Bid), bonding requirements, and department-specific procedures are assumed knowledge
- **Not knowing what to do first** — even motivated businesses don't know where to start

Richmond's current procurement portal lists hundreds of opportunities, but it was built for vendors who **already know the system**. Most minority-owned businesses and first-time contractors don't have that background.

> "That gap is exactly what Pillar 4 highlights."

---

## Solution

**Richmond Contract Navigator** is a simple, AI-powered tool that helps minority-owned and first-time businesses in Richmond understand which city contracting opportunities might fit them — **in plain English**.

---

## Demo Walkthrough

### Step 1 — Describe Your Business in Plain English
- The user describes their business in plain language — no jargon required
- Example used: **Lotus Love LLC** — a software, web design, and AI training company in Virginia (Mia's own company)
- An optional field allows the user to enhance the business description; default values are pre-filled

### Step 2 — Click "Matching Contracts"
- The system queries an **open city dataset** of procurement opportunities
- An AI enrichment layer processes the query in the background ("does some magic behind the scenes")
- Within seconds, results are returned

### Step 3 — Email Alerts (Optional)
- Users can enter their email to receive **similar alerts in the future** when new matching contracts are posted
- Adding an email signature is all that is required

### Step 4 — Review Results
Results are categorized by match strength:
- **Strong Match**
- **Strong Match**
- **Good Match**

For each result, the display includes:
- **Why it matched** — plain-language explanation of the connection to the business
- **What it's about** — description of the contract
- **Funding amount** — how much the contract is worth
- **Contract type**
- **Closing date**
- **Vendor Readiness Checklist** — what the business needs to have in place to actually bid on this contract

### Step 5 — Vendor Readiness Roadmap
A general **vendor readiness roadmap** is available for all users — outlining the steps any business needs to take to become a qualified city vendor, regardless of which specific contracts they're targeting.

### Step 6 — Glossary and Data Access
- A built-in **glossary** explains key procurement terms (e.g., IFB, bonding, etc.) in plain English
- Users can **open the underlying datasets** directly from within the tool for full transparency into the data source

---

## What It Changes

Richmond's procurement portal was built for experienced vendors. Richmond Contract Navigator is built for **everyone else** — the first-time contractor who doesn't know what an IFB is, the minority business owner who has never navigated bonding requirements, the entrepreneur who just needs someone to say "here's where you start."
