# TradePath RVA — Pathway to Skilled Trades Careers

**Pillar:** Thriving Economy — Connecting Underrepresented Communities to High-Opportunity Trades Careers
**Key Partner:** People's Advantage Federal Credit Union
**Funding Commitment:** $50,000 from People's Advantage FCU, leveraging a city $5,000 pilot investment
**Tagline:** Discover. Prepare. Persist.
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Context: Who This Is For

Richmond has **53,000 workers employed in skilled trades jobs**. These are not poverty wages — these are **$40,000, $50,000, and $60,000 careers**. Each year there are thousands of openings, and the people who could fill them are already here in Richmond — one barrier away from changing their life.

The team started not by building a product, but by asking a question:

> "What's actually in the way?"

And then they listened.

---

## The Real Barriers (Heard Directly from Residents)

- Six months of training is a long time when you're already living close to the financial edge
- **Daycare doesn't pause** while you're in school
- You **can't buy the boots until you have the job**, but you can't get the job without the boots
- Transportation to training programs
- Wages lost while in class
- Tools of the trade — upfront costs with no income yet

Traditional resources like grants and scholarships exist, but they **don't cover these gap costs** — the boots, the childcare, the transportation, the lost wages.

---

## Solution

**TradePath RVA** is a mobile-first platform that meets people where they are — on their phone, in their language, at whatever hour they finally have a minute to breathe.

### Core Features

#### 1. Explore Available Trades
Users can browse available skilled trades paths and see for each one:
- What you'll **earn** in this career
- What's **required** to enter it
- What it **costs** to get there
- **How to pay for it** — funding sources, grants, scholarships

This is explicitly described as *"not a brochure"* — it is a **personalized path to a new career**.

#### 2. Barrier Assessment
TradePath RVA does something most resources don't: it **asks what's in the way**.

The system asks users directly about their barriers:
- Do you need childcare?
- Do you need transportation?
- Do you need help with tools of the trade?
- How will you cover wages lost while in class?

The purpose is not to screen people out — it is to **understand what they need so TradePath can deliver a solution that addresses all their barriers**.

---

## The Funding Innovation: Trade Pathway Revolving Loan Fund

In partnership with **People's Advantage Federal Credit Union**, TradePath RVA introduces a novel **public-private funding solution** designed specifically for the gap costs that traditional funding sources miss.

### Structure
- **People's Advantage FCU** has committed to investing **$50,000** towards community impact
- This leverages a **city investment of $5,000** to pilot the program
- The mechanism: **The Trade Pathway Revolving Loan Fund**

### Loan Terms
- Loan amount: **$500 to $2,500** — sized for real gap costs (boots, childcare deposit, tools)
- **No credit score required**
- Loans are originated and serviced by People's Advantage Federal Credit Union
- **Repayment starts after the borrower lands the job** — not before

> "Because we know they will be successful."

## Why This Can Work

Richmond already has everything it needs:
- The **training programs** exist
- The **employers** exist and have openings
- The **credit union** is committed and capitalized
- The **residents** want the jobs

TradePath RVA provides the **connecting system** — the infrastructure that links all of these pieces together for the person standing at the door who just needs someone to say yes.

## Design Philosophy

> "TradePath RVA was built for you — the person who may not know where to start. It meets you where you are on your phone, in your language, at whatever hour you finally have a minute to breathe."

The platform is explicitly built to be **mobile-first**, **multilingual**, and accessible at any time — because the people it serves don't have the luxury of business hours.
