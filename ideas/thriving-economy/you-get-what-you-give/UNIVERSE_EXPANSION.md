# From Hackathon to Hub: Scaling "You Get What You Give" into a Regional B2B Engine

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

"You Get What You Give RVA" (YGWYG) represents a critical shift from passive business directories to active economic matchmaking. While born as a Richmond-specific hackathon project, its true potential lies in its ability to serve as a "Civic CRM" for the entire Greater Richmond region. By leveraging the Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC) data as a foundational layer and applying AI-driven "mutual benefit" logic, the tool addresses a systemic gap: the high cost of discovery for small businesses. 

To move beyond a prototype, YGWYG must transition from a city-owned utility to a nonprofit-led regional platform. This expansion unlocks high-tier funding from GO Virginia and the EDA, which prioritize regional collaboration over municipal silos. The strategic "North Star" for this project is not just to list businesses, but to prove incremental local spend—transforming the "give first" philosophy into a measurable increase in the regional multiplier effect.

## 2. EXPANDED OWNERSHIP RECOMMENDATION

**Recommended Model: Hybrid City-Enabled, Nonprofit-Owned**

Municipal procurement and IT maintenance cycles are too slow for LLM-based tools. Successful civic tech often fails when "handed off" to city IT. Therefore, the project should adopt a hybrid model:
* **What the City should own:** The data pipeline. The City of Richmond should act as the "Data Anchor," formalizing the "Clean Richmond Business Feed" as an API via its Open Data portal.
* **What partners should own:** The product and user experience. A regional nonprofit like Startup Virginia or Activation Capital should own, host, and iterate the software.
* **What should NOT be city-owned:** The AI-generated "mutual benefit" statements. City ownership of AI hallucinations carries unnecessary liability and political risk.

## 5. EXISTING EFFORTS / PRIOR ART

* **Yiftee / Factor4 (National):**
 * *What:* Closed-loop community gift cards used in 500+ communities to support local businesses [30] [31].
 * *Lesson:* Proved that "Buy Local" only works when the technology is seamless. YGWYG must ensure its output is highly actionable.
* **NYC Open Streets (Mastercard Study):**
 * *What:* Used data-driven impact measurement to assess the effectiveness of public sector change [32] [33].
 * *Lesson:* Data-backed proof of "incremental spend" is the only way to sustain long-term political and philanthropic support.
* **Colu "City Coins" & BerkShares:**
 * *What:* Rewarded residents with local tokens for civic engagement; local currencies.
 * *Lesson:* Failed when the "currency" became too complex or showed zero macroeconomic impact. YGWYG must avoid tokenization and focus on B2B connections.

## 6. SCALE SHIFT ANALYSIS

* **The "Richmond-Only" Trap:** Framed only as a Richmond project, the tool is a "nice-to-have" directory that misses the broader regional business activity.
* **The Regional Power-Up:** By including Henrico and Chesterfield, the tool becomes a true Supply Chain Tool. A Richmond IT firm needs the data centers in Henrico and the manufacturers in Chesterfield. Regionality turns a "directory" into a "market map" and unlocks GO Virginia funding [1].
* **The State-Level Vision:** If the SCC adopted this logic, YGWYG could become the "Front Door" for all 3.5M Virginia businesses. The City of Richmond's role is to pilot the logic so the State can eventually scale the infrastructure.

## 7. RISK AND CONSTRAINT ANALYSIS

* **Data Decay:** SCC data is a snapshot. Without a live API connection, the "14,000 active businesses" will quickly become inaccurate.
* **The "Hallucination" Liability:** Claude-generated "mutual benefit" statements are high-value but prone to error. A single bad recommendation erodes professional trust.
* **The "Informal Economy" Blindspot:** SCC data only captures registered entities, systematically excluding the "solopreneurs" prevalent in underserved communities.
* **Procurement Bottlenecks:** If the City tries to "buy" this software, the RFP process could kill momentum.
