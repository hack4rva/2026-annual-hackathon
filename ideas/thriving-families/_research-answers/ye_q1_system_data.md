# Executive Summary

The City of Richmond, Virginia, facilitates youth employment through programs like Youth Works RVA and the Mayor's Youth Employment Summit (also known as Mayor's Youth Academy), targeting youth aged 14-24. The state's Virginia Electronic Employment Certificate System (VAeECS) for work permits involves a three-part online submission (youth, employer, parent) followed by a Department of Labor & Industry (DOLI) review, which generally occurs within 24 hours of complete submission. The final certificate is issued electronically to the employer and becomes valid only after the youth signs it. While program goals and application processes are public, specific performance metrics are not. Research found no publicly available data for 2025 or 2026 on the number of teen applicants, acceptance rates, or stage-by-stage dropout rates for these programs. Similarly, there is no machine-readable list of participating employer worksites available on the city's open data portal, nor are there published statistics on the percentage of Richmond employers who use E-Verify. Teens discover these opportunities through official channels like the city's Youth Engagement Services (YES) website, social media, virtual information sessions, and community partner announcements.

# Work Permit Process Details

## System Name

VAeECS (Virginia Electronic Employment Certificate System)

## Process Description

The process is a three-part electronic registration that begins after a youth has a firm job offer. First, the youth completes their registration. Second, the employer completes the employer section. Third, the parent or guardian completes their respective section. Only after all three electronic sections are completed is the application submitted to the Department of Labor & Industry (DOLI) for review.

## Review Timeline

Once all three electronic sections are submitted, a DOLI Compliance Officer generally reviews the application within 24 hours. If approved, the Employment Certificate is issued electronically to the employer. The certificate only becomes valid once the youth worker signs it.

# Youth Works Rva Details

Youth Works RVA is the City of Richmond's official eight-week summer workforce development program, managed by the city's Youth Engagement Services, which is also known as the Mayor’s Youth Academy. The program is designed to engage young people from Richmond in career exploration primarily through work-based learning experiences. The core mission is to provide participants with a combination of virtual learning and practical, hands-on experience through internships and job placements. According to a partner announcement, the program offers paid work experiences and professional development opportunities across the city. For the 2026 cycle, the Richmond Office of Community Wealth Building aims to serve 750 participants. The success of this annual program relies on a collaborative effort between local government agencies, private sector employers, community-based organizations, and small businesses that volunteer to serve as partner employers and provide structured job placements for the youth.
