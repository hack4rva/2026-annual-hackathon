# Executive Summary

Research into youth employment equity in Richmond, Virginia, reveals several significant barriers and systemic issues. A primary procedural hurdle is Virginia's work permit requirement for 14-15 year olds, which mandates notarized consent from a parent, guardian, or custodian. This presents a substantial obstacle for youth in foster care, unaccompanied minors, or those with absent parents, as the law provides no alternative signer. The state's electronic work permit system, VAeECS, operates as a closed portal, requiring separate, independent actions from the youth, employer, and guardian, with no functionality for pre-population or integration with external support tools. Access to specialized programs is also a challenge; for instance, Richmond youth with disabilities seeking to participate in Project SEARCH are routed to suburban host sites in Henrico, Hanover, or Chesterfield, as there is no dedicated site within the city. Furthermore, while Richmond's youth curfew includes an exception for travel to and from work, 2018 police data revealed that 98% of juveniles stopped for curfew violations were Black, indicating a significant racial disparity in enforcement that could deter employment. Significant data gaps prevent a full understanding of the landscape; there are no published statistics on the percentage of Richmond teens with government-issued IDs or lacking vital records, nor is there publicly available data identifying census tracts with high concentrations of youth Not in Employment, Education, or Training (NEET) or a detailed breakdown of the 60+ languages spoken by Richmond Public Schools families by neighborhood. A positive legislative change set for July 1, 2025, will waive fees for vital records and state IDs for youth experiencing homelessness, addressing one known financial barrier.

# Work Permit Process For Vulnerable Youth

For teens aged 14-15 in Virginia, an employment certificate requires permission from a 'parent, guardian, or custodian,' as specified by the Department of Labor and Industry (DOLI). For youth in non-traditional family structures, this is navigated as follows: In the case of youth in foster care, the legal custodian, such as the local department of social services (LDSS) or a foster parent as defined by the custody order, is authorized to sign the consent forms. However, the law presents a significant barrier for unaccompanied minors who do not have an available parent, guardian, or custodian. The Virginia code does not provide for an alternative signer in this scenario. This means that before a work permit can be obtained, a legal custodian or guardian must typically be established through a court or agency process.

# Infrastructure Gaps Broadband And Transit

## Broadband Gap Analysis

Analysis of broadband internet service gaps in Richmond can be conducted using the 'Commonwealth Connection' broadband map. This tool, provided by the Virginia Department of Housing and Community Development (DHCD), allows users to view broadband coverage at the census block level. It provides data on which areas have access to critical internet infrastructure and allows for comparison between Virginia's own data and the FCC's address-level broadband maps. This resource can be used to identify specific low-coverage census blocks and tracts within the city.

## Public Transit Gap Analysis

Analysis of public transportation service gaps can be performed using the Greater Richmond Transit Company (GRTC) system map and the Transit Strategic Plan (TSP). These documents identify existing bus routes, service frequency, and areas with limited or no service. By examining these resources, one can identify corridors and neighborhoods within Richmond that are underserved by public transit, which is a potential barrier for youth accessing employment and educational opportunities.

## Overlap Analysis Finding

The research did not find a published, official analysis confirming a direct overlap between census tracts with high concentrations of NEET youth and those with significant broadband or public transit infrastructure gaps. While the tools to map the infrastructure gaps exist (Commonwealth Connection for broadband, GRTC maps for transit), the prerequisite data identifying tract-level NEET 'hot spots' is not publicly available. Therefore, while these tools would support such an analysis, no definitive, published conclusion on this specific overlap was located.

# Va Eecs Portal Functionality

## Portal Name

Virginia Electronic Employment Certificate System

## External Integration Supported

False

## Process Workflow

The VAeECS portal requires a sequential, multi-party workflow that cannot be initiated or pre-populated by an external system. The process mandates separate, independent actions by each party. First, the youth must register and receive a 'Youth Unique Identifier' number. Second, the youth provides this number to both the employer and their parent/guardian/custodian. Third, the employer and the parent/guardian/custodian must each use this identifier to log in separately and complete their respective sections. The system does not support APIs or deep-linking for pre-population; external tools can only direct users to the DOLI portal to begin this manual, multi-step process.


# Key Barriers Identified

## Barrier Category

Procedural / Legal

## Specific Issue

Virginia's work permit process for 14-15 year olds requires notarized consent from a 'parent, guardian, or custodian.' The law does not provide an alternative signer for unaccompanied minors or youth without an available legal guardian, creating a significant legal barrier to employment that requires establishing guardianship before a permit can be obtained.

## Barrier Category

Documentation / Financial

## Specific Issue

Youth, particularly those experiencing homelessness, face challenges in obtaining necessary identification like a Virginia Child ID or vital records such as a birth certificate and Social Security card. While specific data on the number of affected youth is unavailable, the passage of a law to waive fees for these documents for homeless youth starting July 1, 2025, acknowledges this as a significant barrier.

## Barrier Category

Technology / Procedural

## Specific Issue

The Virginia Electronic Employment Certificate System (VAeECS) requires the youth, employer, and parent/guardian to each independently register, log in, and complete their section of the application. The system does not support pre-population of data or integration with external tools, adding a layer of complexity and potential points of failure to the application process.

## Barrier Category

Systemic / Equity

## Specific Issue

There is a severe racial disparity in the enforcement of Richmond's youth curfew. Despite an exception for travel to and from work, 2018 data showed that 98% of juveniles stopped for curfew violations were Black. This disproportionate enforcement can create a chilling effect on employment for youth of color, particularly for late-shift jobs.

## Barrier Category

Access to Services / Geographic

## Specific Issue

Youth with disabilities in Richmond Public Schools do not have access to a city-based Project SEARCH site. The program's host sites for the metro area are located in the surrounding counties of Chesterfield, Henrico, and Hanover, requiring city youth to be routed to suburban locations for this specific work-transition program.


# Minor Worker Protections In Virginia

## Protection Measure

The primary protection for young workers in Virginia is the requirement of an Employment Certificate. This certificate is not valid unless a parent, guardian, or custodian grants permission, a process which can be done electronically via the VAeECS system or through a notarized manual form.

## Applicable Age Group

The Employment Certificate requirement specifically applies to minors who are 14 and 15 years old.

## Governing Body

The Virginia Department of Labor and Industry (DOLI) is the state agency responsible for overseeing youth employment laws and issuing the Employment Certificates through its electronic (VAeECS) or manual processes.


# Richmond City Curfew Ordinance

## Curfew Hours

The curfew is in effect from 11 p.m. to 5 a.m.

## Applicable Minors

The curfew applies to unemancipated minors under the age of 18.

## Allowed Exceptions

A minor is exempt from the curfew if they are with a parent or guardian, or if they are traveling to or from work, school, religious activities, or civic organization activities.
