# Richmond Youth Hub

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** Thriving Families  
**Problem Statement:** Give Richmond youth one clear place to explore summer jobs, internships, and first-job guidance.

---

## Description

A **unified youth employment navigator** for Richmond: one portal aggregating summer jobs, internships, and workforce programs from city agencies, nonprofits, and employers, with search, application support, and placement tracking — modeled on large-city consolidated systems referenced in the analysis. **Core assumption challenged:** Richmond only needs a better job board; research summarized says directory-only models underperform versus platforms with centralized intake, employer tooling, and sometimes payroll or training layers. Richmond youth currently navigate many separate sites and forms, lowering discovery and duplicating paperwork.

## Key Approach

- Youth-facing discovery and application flow with shared profile data routing to eligible programs; employer posting and review where scope allows.
- Integrated onboarding module: work permits, orientation, transit — aligning with SMS-first or document-kit companion ideas in the same pillar.
- Governance and partnerships called out as harder than the tech: data-sharing agreements, common listing schema, backbone funding and ownership.
- Weekend MVP shaped as curated aggregated listings and simple intake to **prove the discovery gap**, not pretend full payroll integration.
- Outcome reporting for funders (placements, completion, demographics) as a sustained-program requirement in the analysis.
