# Stepwise — Youth Employment Platform

**Presenters:** Nana and Gabriel
**Pillar:** Thriving Families
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Young adults in the Richmond area lack a job board that accounts for their age, legal eligibility, parental oversight requirements, and practical constraints like transportation. Existing platforms don't distinguish between a 14-year-old who needs parental consent and a 20-year-old who can apply independently.

---

## Solution

**Stepwise** connects Richmond youth ages 14–24 to real work opportunities with support, prep, and guidance built in. The platform serves three distinct user flows: **parents** of supervised minors, **candidates** (self-service), and **employers**.

---

## Parent Flow

### Student Profile Management
- Parents create accounts and set up profiles for their students (ages 14–15)
- Each profile captures availability and transportation details
- The platform aims for COPPA compliance — parents explicitly grant access to personalize their teen's experience

### Invite Key System
- After creating a student profile, the parent generates an **invite key**
- The teen uses this key to access their own portal, linked to the parent's supervisory account

### Job Readiness Checklist
- Parents can review and verify their teen's **readiness checklist** — confirming documents like ID, work permit, resume, and bank account are in order before any applications go out

### Application Approval
- When a supervised teen applies for a job, the parent receives a notification and must **approve the application** before it's submitted
- The demo showed Nana applying for a data analyst role at $49.99/hr, with the parent reviewing and approving it

---

## Candidate Flow (Self-Service)

- Candidates ages 16–24 sign up independently without parental oversight
- Access to the same job board, readiness checklist, and resources
- Applications go straight through without an approval step

---

## Employer Flow

### Verification Gate
- Employers must be **verified** before they can post jobs
- Unverified employers see a dashboard but cannot create listings — demonstrated by logging in with an unverified account first

### Dashboard
- Verified employers see:
  - **Active postings** count
  - **Total applicants**
  - **Positions filled**

### Hiring Pipeline
- New applicants appear on the dashboard
- Employers can review readiness status and move candidates through stages: **review → offer → hired**
- The demo showed Gabriel reviewing an applicant, making an offer, and marking as hired

### Analytics
- Employer analytics page shows applicant interaction data for each job posting

### Youth Hiring Guide
- A built-in **resource guide** for hiring youth in Virginia
- Tailored to Richmond and Virginia state labor laws

---

## Company Profile
- Employers maintain a company profile visible to candidates

---

## End-to-End Demo Highlight

The demo closed the loop: after the employer (Gabriel) extended an offer, the parent portal (Nana) showed the student's application status updated to **hired**, and the job listing was no longer available to apply to — confirming the full lifecycle from application through parental approval to hire.

---

## Key Differentiators

- **Three-flow architecture** — parent-supervised, self-service candidate, and employer — rather than a single generic job board
- **COPPA-conscious design** for minors under 16
- **Invite key system** linking parent and teen accounts
- **Virginia labor law compliance** built into the employer experience
- **Readiness checklist** ensures teens are prepared before applying
