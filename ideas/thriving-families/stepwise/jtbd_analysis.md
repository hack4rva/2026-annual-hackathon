# Project Analysis Summary

The 'stepwise' project is a civic technology initiative for Richmond, VA, operating under the 'Thriving Families' pillar. It aims to address the problem of fragmented youth employment pathways by creating a single, clear digital resource for local youth to explore summer jobs, internships, and guidance for their first job. The core goal of the associated Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) analysis is to deeply understand the needs, motivations, and pain points of the entire ecosystem—including young job seekers (ages 14-24), their parents/guardians, and the city's program coordinators. This analysis seeks to inform the development of the 'stepwise' product, ensuring it provides practical, step-by-step guidance that helps teens successfully navigate the entire employment journey, from discovering opportunities with programs like Youth Works RVA to completing necessary administrative tasks like securing work permits and arranging transportation.

# Functional Job Statement

## Situation

When summer approaches and I am trying to land my first paid summer role

## Persona

a Richmond high school student (14–18)

## Motivation

to find one place to see local programs (like YES/Youth Works RVA, community employers, nonprofits) and understand exactly what to do next, such as applying, securing references, preparing a resume, getting a work permit if I'm 14-15, and arranging transportation

## Outcome

I can start work on time and gain real experience

## Current Workaround

Asking counselors and teachers, searching social media, and navigating between multiple websites like RVA.gov, school pages, and individual employer sites.

## Core Pain

The information is scattered and the steps and timelines are unclear, which means I might miss deadlines and fail to complete all requirements, like getting a work permit at the right time.


# Emotional Trust Job Statement

## Situation

When I see my teen applying for jobs

## Persona

a parent/guardian in Richmond

## Motivation

to find trusted, city-aligned guidance on safe, vetted opportunities with clear age limits, hours, and program credibility, as well as transparent steps for permits, expectations, and pay

## Outcome

I can feel confident my child is safe, fairly treated, and building toward a future pathway

## Current Workaround

Relying on word-of-mouth or school bulletins and hoping the opportunities are legitimate.

## Core Pain

Anxiety over my child's safety, the legality of the job under child labor rules, and the quality of the program.


# Systems Coordination Job Statement

## Situation

When I am preparing for summer placements

## Persona

a city or school workforce coordinator or an employer partner in Richmond

## Motivation

to have a shared, streamlined intake process and a clear readiness signal from youth applicants, including their eligibility, required documents, transportation plan, and basic job readiness

## Outcome

I can match more teens to positions quickly, reduce onboarding friction, and improve program completion rates

## Current Workaround

Using separate application forms across different programs, managing communication through email chains, and performing manual eligibility checks.

## Core Pain

Duplicative data collection and late-stage disqualifications due to youth missing necessary permits, IDs, or viable transportation plans, which slows down the entire placement process.

# Existing Richmond Programs And Stakeholders

## Name

Youth Works RVA

## Managing Entity

Youth Engagement Services (YES), which is the youth-serving arm of the City of Richmond's Office of Community Wealth Building (OCWB).

## Target Audience

Youth aged 14 to 24 who are residents of the City of Richmond or are actively enrolled in foster care services. The program features distinct tracks tailored to different age groups, such as the 'Virtual Earn and Learn Program' for 14-15 year olds and the 'In-Person Program' for 16-24 year olds.

## Key Services

The program is an eight-week summer workforce development initiative focused on career exploration through work-based learning. Key services include hands-on experience through internships and job placements, job readiness training, leadership development, and entrepreneurship. The Summer 2024 program involved 502 youth participants and 85 employer partners.


# Identified Systemic Barriers And Opportunities

## Category

Barrier

## Description

A complex, multi-step work permit process mandated by the state of Virginia for youth aged 14 and 15. According to the Virginia Department of Labor and Industry (DOLI), a youth in this age group cannot be issued an Employment Certificate (work permit) until they have already secured a firm job offer from an employer. The process requires registration in an electronic system by the youth, the employer, and a parent/guardian.

## Impact On Youth

This procedural sequence creates a significant point of confusion and a potential roadblock for first-time job seekers. They face a 'chicken-and-egg' problem where they need a job to get a permit, but the steps to get the permit while securing the job are not intuitive. This can lead to incomplete applications, missed deadlines, and late-stage disqualification from opportunities, ultimately preventing them from starting a job on time or at all.
