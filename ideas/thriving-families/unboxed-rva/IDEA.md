## Problem Statement

Richmond lacks a tech tool connecting youth to employment opportunities. But the team didn't stop at the obvious framing — they immediately challenged the premise of what "youth employment" should look like.

### The Youth Side
Traditional work experiences for young people are broken:
> "Youth don't want an 8-week internship alphabetizing files in a back closet. They want meaningful work — work that matches how they think and see the world."

Gen Z and those after them are entering a workforce where gig work will be the norm. The tools being built for them should reflect that reality, not the one from 20 years ago.

### The Business Side
Richmond is growing and small businesses are opening at record rates — but there is no easy way for them to find help for the specific, real work they actually need done.

### The Gap
Two groups of Richmonders who could genuinely help each other are not connecting.

## Solution: Unboxed RVA

**Unboxed RVA** is a project marketplace connecting Richmond youth with paid, project-based work from local small businesses.

### How It Works for Businesses
1. Businesses post **projects** — not jobs, not internships — with:
   - A clear brief
   - A defined timeline
   - A real, tangible deliverable
2. Examples of the kinds of projects the platform is designed for:
   - Steaming inventory and writing product descriptions for a consignment store
   - Producing promotional reels ahead of a restaurant's second location opening
3. Once posted, the platform surfaces aligned candidates — not just by interest, but by fit

### How It Works for Youth ("Seekers")
1. A young person creates a simple profile:
   - Name, birthday, email, home address, school
   - Because many users are minors, **parental consent is built directly into the experience** — making it secure and easy to complete
2. The seeker selects the type of work they're interested in:
   - Design and content creation
   - Business support
   - Coding
   - Fashion
   - Food and hospitality
3. They share any experience they already have — even if it comes from helping family, posting online, volunteering, or self-taught skills
4. From there, the platform connects them to **real opportunities nearby with local Richmond businesses**

### The Reimagined Process
Seekers move through a completely reimagined version of the traditional employment process:
- **Explore projects** — review what's required and what they'll learn
- **Select a Connect call time** — when interested, they pick a time to speak with the employer (keeping the process simple)
- **RIA guides them** — the AI assistant helps the seeker understand the opportunity, prepare for the conversation, and build confidence
- **Confirmed Connect call** — time and details sent directly to the seeker
- **Onboarding** — expectations, location details, and next steps
- **Dashboard** — track progress, check-ins, and deliverables in one place
- **Completion** — see what they completed, what they earned, and the skills they built

### For Employers
RIA also supports businesses in creating projects:
1. Business describes what they need
2. **RIA shapes the project** to be youth-friendly, clear, and realistic
3. Employer outlines tools, skills, pay, and support the role needs
4. Project goes live; Unboxed surfaces aligned candidates
5. Employer reviews profiles, schedules an intro, and moves forward with confidence
6. At project completion, **feedback is captured, growth is recognized**, and the door stays open for what comes next

## RIA — The Intelligence Layer

RIA is Unboxed's AI guide — described as "a warm guide supporting the student throughout the journey." RIA serves both sides:

- **For youth:** Helps them understand opportunities, prepare for conversations, and build confidence
- **For employers:** Helps shape project briefs to be youth-accessible and realistic
- **Translation layer:** Helps both sides understand how the other speaks, thinks, and operates
