# 804Me — Community Resource Navigation Hub

**Pillar:** Thriving Inclusive Communities
**Tagline:** Find your community today.
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## The Problem

> "Richmond, Virginia — a city of over 200,000 stories. Many of our city's vital resources are locked behind language barriers and complex paperwork. Finding help shouldn't be a struggle."

Despite hundreds of community resources existing in Richmond, residents who need them most often cannot find or access them. Resources are scattered, language barriers block access, and the digital divide excludes people who aren't tech-savvy.

---

## Solution: 804Me

A **two-component system** combining physical infrastructure (the 804Me Hub) with a digital platform (the 804Me web app) — designed to serve residents who need help right now, with zero friction and zero data collection.

---

## Component 1: The 804Me Physical Hub

A **24/7 physical gateway** placed in the hearts of Richmond's neighborhoods, transit centers, and identified resource gaps. The hubs:
- Never close their doors
- Are mapped to locations where they are needed most
- Provide printed resources with **QR codes** that instantly unlock the full web app experience on any device

> "We're putting the heart of our city back into the hands of the people of Richmond."

The printed ticket from the hub is described as a **bridge** — it doesn't leave the resident with a piece of paper, it connects them to the full digital experience via QR scan.

---

## Component 2: The 804Me Web App

### Core Design Principles
- **No account required** — zero registration
- **No personal data collected** — completely anonymous
- **Zero data tracking**
- Available in **English, Spanish, and Arabic** with a single click to switch

### Navigator Profile System
The app uses **navigator profiles** — diverse avatars that represent different community backgrounds and experiences:
- Residents can select a navigator that matches their background (e.g., Latinx, family with children, elderly, immigrant)
- Selecting a navigator **instantly narrows** the map and resource list to what's most relevant to that profile
- Profiles help curate the map without requiring the user to disclose personal information

### Interactive Local Map
The centerpiece of the web app:
- A fully interactive map loaded with community resources in the user's area
- Resources include: housing, emergency shelter, food assistance, and more
- Every pin on the map shows a **pop-up with details** about that specific resource

### Sidebar Navigation
The left sidebar provides:
- **Organizations tab** — scroll through localized organizations by category (housing, emergency, shelter, food)
- **Hubs tab** — locate central community hubs and resource hubs near the user
- Service providers can update their own organization's information or manage the resources they offer through this tab

### Quick Search Buttons
- Fast-access buttons at the top for common needs
- Example: Clicking "Food Assistance" instantly narrows the map to local food pantries and community meal services

### Anonymity as a Feature
The entire experience is deliberately anonymous:
> "We help you connect with your community without ever requiring an account or personal data."

This is especially important for residents who may be undocumented, in crisis, or who distrust systems that collect data about them.

---

## Accessibility

- Full platform available in **Spanish** and **Arabic** — single-click language switching
- Zero tech barriers — works on any device, accessible from a QR code at a physical hub
- Designed for residents who may be new to the city, facing a crisis, or navigating systems for the first time
