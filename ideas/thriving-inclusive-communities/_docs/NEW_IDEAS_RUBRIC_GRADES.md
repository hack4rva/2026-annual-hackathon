## PS2 — Cross-Agency Service Navigation

### Idea 14: Reentry 72-Hour Navigator

| Category | Score | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Impact | 5 | The first 72 hours post-release is the highest-attrition window. Structured guidance during this period addresses the most critical moment in the reentry journey. |
| Feasibility | 4 | Step-by-step guide with offline caching is a proven mobile pattern. Data sources (OAR, DSS, VCW, GRTC) are all public. |
| User Value | 5 | Answers the exact question a newly released person asks: "What do I do right now?" Actionable, sequential, specific to Richmond. |
| Execution | 4 | Mobile guide with offline capability, agency info, and bus routes is demo-able. Co-design with OAR adds credibility. |
| Innovation | 3 | Step-by-step reentry guides exist nationally. Novel for Richmond with local agency specificity and transit integration. |
| Equity | 5 | Targets disproportionately Black men navigating reentry. Includes bus routes for transit-dependent users. Offline-capable for device-unstable population. |

**Pillar Award:** (5×5) + (5×4) + (4×3) + (3×3) + (4×3) + (5×3) = 25 + 20 + 12 + 9 + 12 + 15 = **93**
**Mayor's Choice:** (5×5) + (4×5) + (5×4) + (4×3) + (5×3) + (3×1) = 25 + 20 + 20 + 12 + 15 + 3 = **95**

### Idea 13: Core Eligibility Pack Builder

| Category | Score | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Impact | 5 | Directly reduces story-repetition across agencies — the exact PS2 problem. Creates a reusable document that works across DSS, VCW, RRHA. |
| Feasibility | 4 | Public eligibility rules are available. Guided form + PDF output is a standard build. Translations in 3 languages achievable. |
| User Value | 5 | Resident walks away with a reusable document they control. Immediate practical value at the next agency visit. |
| Execution | 4 | Guided form + PDF generation + QR code is demo-able in 48 hours. Field mapping across 3 agencies requires research but is scoped. |
| Innovation | 4 | Client-controlled data portability approach — legally bypasses MOU and BAA walls. Novel framing of "resident as data controller." |
| Equity | 4 | Multilingual. Accessible on phone. But requires literacy to complete the form — navigator assistance recommended for low-literacy users. |

**Pillar Award:** (5×5) + (5×4) + (4×3) + (4×3) + (4×3) + (4×3) = 25 + 20 + 12 + 12 + 12 + 12 = **93**
**Mayor's Choice:** (5×5) + (4×5) + (5×4) + (4×3) + (4×3) + (4×1) = 25 + 20 + 20 + 12 + 12 + 4 = **93**

## PS1 — Immigrant Service Discovery

### Idea 1: WhatsApp Service Finder Bot (Signpost-Lite)

| Category | Score | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Impact | 4 | Reaches users in the channel they already trust (WhatsApp); directly addresses discovery gap. Does not solve the underlying data fragmentation. |
| Feasibility | 4 | WhatsApp Business API is well-documented; canned response bot with interactive buttons is buildable in a weekend. Requires WhatsApp Business account approval. |
| User Value | 5 | Meets residents where they are — no new platform adoption, no downloads, no accounts. Human handoff for complex queries adds real safety net. |
| Execution | 4 | Working bot with 10 intent categories demo-able in 48 hours. Human inbox setup (Zendesk/Front) adds complexity but is documented. |
| Innovation | 3 | Signpost-lite pattern is well-documented prior art but has never been deployed in Richmond. Adaptation, not invention. |
| Equity | 5 | Reaches smartphone-dependent, prepaid-data users. Works without broadband. Multilingual buttons remove literacy barrier. WhatsApp is the preferred channel for immigrant communities per OIRE. |
