## The Core Philosophy of Pollinators for Change

The organization's entire framework begins with three foundational questions:

**Question 1:** What is the source of every single social challenge?
> Answer: People don't have what they need to be okay.

**Question 2:** What do people need to be okay?
> Answer: Resources — ranging from food and water to housing, education, and healthcare, all the way up to healthy relationships and a sense of purpose.

**Question 3:** How do people access the resources they need to be okay?
> Answer: Coordinated infrastructure.

This reasoning is the direct foundation for Circle Trust.

## Pollinators for Change — What It Is

Pollinators for Change is built on a **regenerative governance framework** that activates intrinsically motivated change makers in communities across **49 sectors of impact** — enabling action on virtually any systemic challenge facing a community.

As of the hackathon, the organization has activated **over 300 pollinators** across the Richmond City and Metro region.

---

## Circle Trust — What It Is

**Circle Trust** is the **digital coordination infrastructure** that pollinators need to activate at scale across Richmond and the surrounding metro region.

It is described explicitly as an **invitation-only network** — not a public platform. The reasons for this:
- Change makers need to be verified as **genuinely in the work**
- **Relational accountability** among pollinators is essential
- Building **trust with immigrant communities** requires knowing who is actually on the platform
- Ensuring participants are authentically committed to solving community and systemic challenges

---

## How Circle Trust Works

After creating an individual user profile, pollinators can activate in two ways:

### 1. Circles
A circle is a **community-led, action-oriented group** organized around a specific issue.

**Live Example Demonstrated:** The **Jackson Ward Graffiti Removal Circle**, being created by Larkin and Ashley during the demo.

**The Problem It Solves:**
Larkin and Ashley are motivated to keep Jackson Ward safe and beautiful by cleaning up graffiti themselves. However, the current process requires going through **city bureaucracy** — which is slow, expensive, and not effective at keeping the community safe and beautiful in a timely way.

**What the Circle Does:**
- Larkin and Ashley start a circle to activate neighbors and the appropriate city contacts
- The circle gives them **access to resources right within their community**
- They can clear graffiti **immediately and effectively** — without waiting on bureaucratic timelines
