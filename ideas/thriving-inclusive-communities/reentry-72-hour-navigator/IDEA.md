# Reentry 72-Hour Navigator

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** Thriving and Inclusive Communities  
**Problem Statement:** Help residents navigate housing, workforce, and reentry services without repeating their story across every agency.

---

## Description

**Core assumption challenged:** reentry support must be comprehensive case management; the analysis argues the strongest evidence clusters around **concrete first-window logistics** — ID, verified referrals, transportation — not holistic platforms alone. Individuals released from Richmond City Jail face a critical **72-hour window** to secure shelter, report to supervision, obtain ID, access food, and arrange transport, without structured, Richmond-specific, time-sequenced guidance. The innovation is a **logistics tool**: printable (and optionally SMS-backed) plans, localized addresses and routes, and navigator views over **verified** provider lists rather than generic national directories.

## Key Approach

- Personalized **printable 72-hour checklist** (release date, parole office, shelter, sequenced tasks); digital companion for navigators with curated provider directory.
- Elevate **pre-release ID** visibility and **immediate transportation** as priority items supported by cited programs and studies.
- Navigator dashboard pattern: curated, verified referrals with time-to-first-referral style metrics vs. cold directory search.
- Partnerships called out: local reentry organizations, jail distribution, GRTC passes or vouchers, shelter capacity reality checks.
- Stretch: SMS reminders, GRTC overlay for key locations, peer navigator concepts — scoped after core checklist validity.
