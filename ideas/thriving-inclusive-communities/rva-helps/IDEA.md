# RVA Helps

**Source:** NEW  
**Pillar:** Thriving and Inclusive Communities  
**Problem Statement:** Help residents safely find and connect to trusted community services without creating accounts or sharing identifying information.

---

## Description

**Core assumption challenged:** the problem is missing directories; the analysis argues directories **exist** but are **stale, fragmented, and measured wrong** — high “closed loop” rates can mask low actual connection to services. Richmond residents, caseworkers, and navigators cannot rely on one accurate, current picture of services across 211, CBOs, and city systems, with no shared standard, freshness discipline, or tracking of whether help was actually received. The reframe is a **resource data utility**: HSDS-aligned data, federation with 211 where possible, **AI-assisted deduplication**, active verification cadence, and **Successful Connection Rate** (not vanity referral counts) as the north star.

## Key Approach

- Unified Richmond service layer: baseline from 211 National Data Platform / Virginia feeds where accessible; merge and dedupe against CBO lists; provider self-update workflows.
- Search UI with **freshness badges**, eligibility and language fields, and user flagging with resolution discipline.
- Referral logging plus follow-up (e.g., SMS) asking whether the client received service, feeding SCR analytics.
- Embeddable widgets for partner sites so discovery lives inside existing intake workflows.
- Sustainability called out explicitly: maintenance funding and stewardship model (e.g., grant conditions for data updates) as primary risk.
