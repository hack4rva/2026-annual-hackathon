# Affordable Housing Compliance Dashboard

**Presenter:** Corey (with teammates Fres and Morelli)
**Pillar:** Thriving Neighborhoods — Affordable Housing Compliance Tracking
**Audience:** City of Richmond Staff
**Hackathon:** Hack for RVA

---

## Problem Statement

Every year, the City of Richmond must track a large number of affordable housing developments and grants to ensure that **performance agreements are staying within compliance**. This is currently a **manual lift** — a time-consuming, staff-heavy process with no unified system for organizing records, tracking commitments, or surfacing upcoming deadlines.

The challenge involves tracking:
- Various affordable housing developments across the city
- Grant performance requirements
- Compliance status of each agreement over time
- Upcoming affordability deadlines

---

## Solution

A **structured compliance dashboard** designed specifically for Richmond city staff — built to simplify day-to-day management of affordable housing records, make compliance status immediately visible, and support decision-making without replacing existing workflows.

### Design Philosophy
> "First and foremost, our focus was really on the staff. How do we make this easy for them with a simple system — but also building a system that can iterate and add whatever they need down the road."

---

## Dashboard Walkthrough

### Top-Line Summary Metrics
The dashboard opens with high-level metrics that staff need at a glance:
- **Active units** — total affordable units currently tracked
- **Total properties** under different agreements (e.g., affordable housing grants)
- **Compliance summary** — aggregate compliance status
- **Active alerts** — anything requiring immediate attention

### Property Status Panel
Scrolling down, staff can see:
- **Property status items** — current state of each tracked property
- **Recent alerts** that have been triggered
- **Upcoming affordability deadlines** — so staff can act before commitments expire

### Click-Through Navigation
Dashboard tiles are **interactive** — clicking any top-line metric takes staff directly to the detailed view for that category.

---

## Compliance Risk Scoring

The compliance view is **risk-scored**:
- Properties are ranked from **most out of compliance → in compliance**
- Provides an immediate visual priority list — staff can see at a glance which properties need the most urgent attention
- Eliminates the need to manually sort through records to identify high-risk items

---

## Alert Management System

The alerts section includes a full workflow for managing compliance issues:
- Staff can **add notes** to any alert
- Staff can **respond** to alerts within the system
- Staff can **acknowledge** alerts to track that action was taken
- Alerts are **categorized** so staff can filter and navigate by type
- When notes are added, they are passed to the relevant category where all communications can be viewed in sequence

---

## HUD Reference Section

A dedicated **HUD reference area** provides:
- Issue information relevant to HUD requirements and standards
- A reference point for staff dealing with federally-funded affordable housing

---

## Developer Cluster View

Staff can also filter to view a **cluster by developer** — allowing them to see all properties or agreements tied to a specific developer at once, useful for portfolio-level compliance reviews.

---

## Planned Integrations

The dashboard is architected to support future additions:
- **Automated alerts** — triggered by system conditions rather than requiring manual monitoring
- **Integration with other city systems** — additional data sources can be piped into the dashboard as needed

---

## Mission

> "Ultimately, this should be something that simplifies things for city staff — helps them meet their mission of staying in compliance with these items, and simplifies the day-to-day tasks of just keeping track of what's going on."
