# Gap Analysis — RVA TRANSFORMERS

**Pillar (submitted):** Thriving Neighborhoods  
**Problem statements for this pillar:** Neighborhood Development Discovery; Affordable Housing Compliance Monitoring  
**Demo:** RVA TRANSFORMERS — RVA 311 Bridge + RVA Biz Navigator  

---

## Alignment Assessment

Neither component addresses **Neighborhood Development Discovery** (finding and understanding development proposals near residents) or **Affordable Housing Compliance Monitoring** (tracking whether publicly funded affordable housing stays affordable over time). The demo frames a unified “navigation” story for residents and entrepreneurs, but that narrative does not map to either Thriving Neighborhoods problem statement.

---

## RVA 311 Bridge

**What it does:** Multilingual, 24/7 interface that mirrors the existing 311 mental model, connects residents to support using trusted city and nonprofit sources, **does not collect or store personal data**, includes a privacy notice up front, and offers the full experience **by phone with no internet required**.

**Pillar fit:** **A Thriving City Hall** — **Resident Service Navigation** (“Help residents find the right City service or department quickly, so requests get routed correctly the first time.”)

---

## RVA Biz Navigator

**What it does:** A single starting point for first-time entrepreneurs with a **four-phase personalized journey**: Legal → Zoning/location (before spending money) → Licensing → Contracting, powered by live city, state, and federal data.

**Pillar fit:** **A Thriving Economy** — **Small Business Navigation** (“Help first-time entrepreneurs understand what steps to take, in what order, to start and register a business in Richmond.”)

---

## Recommendation

This submission should be **re-evaluated under its correct pillar(s)** — City Hall for RVA 311 Bridge and Economy for RVA Biz Navigator (whether as one combined entry or two logical tracks is an organizer/judging policy choice). **For Thriving Neighborhoods pillar scoring, treat the demo as misaligned** with the stated neighborhood problem statements.
