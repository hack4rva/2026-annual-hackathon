Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **City Hall Implementation Group**. You've been brought together because you each played a role in one of Richmond's most pressing civic challenges: making it easier for residents to find the right city service, and helping city staff navigate procurement more effectively. You're here because we think you're the right people to carry these ideas forward.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Daniel Rubio & Priyesh Jain** — built MIRA, this year's Mayor's Choice Award winner. A conversational AI that routes resident service requests before they hit the wrong department.
- **Jonathan & Charlie Trochlil** — built Byrd Park Geese (byrdparkgeese.com), the City Hall Pillar Award winner. A text-based 311 interface — no app required.
- **Baskaran Jeyarajan** — built CivicPulse AI, an AI-powered service navigation layer.
- **Andre Tolleris** — built a Tableau-powered Richmond budget dashboard.
- **Taksheel Shetty** — built Open City Webapp, an open data front-end.
- **Aidan Henn** — built Hey804, a resident assistance tool.
- **Joshua Philip** — built rvaHelp with the Hackstreet Boys team.
- **Ankit Srivastava** (Ithena AI) — built RVA Contract Lens, a procurement search tool for city staff.
- **Sera Davenport** — built MAP Tracker, a progress dashboard for the Mayor's Action Plan.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Letitia Shelton** — Director of Finance, City of Richmond. Controls the financial data and procurement systems that several of these projects depend on.
- **Hal Dowdy** — President, HackRVA. Bridges city IT and Richmond's open-source civic tech community.
- **James Burke** — Co-Founder, South Shore Analytics. Data engineering and visualization consultancy.
