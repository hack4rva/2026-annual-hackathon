Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Neighborhoods Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two of Richmond's most visible housing challenges: helping residents find and engage with development proposals near them before it's too late, and giving city staff real tools to track whether publicly funded affordable housing actually stays affordable over time.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Luke Rabin** (Singlestone Consulting) — built SIMBY (simbyrva.vercel.app), this year's Neighborhoods Pillar Award winner. A development discovery tool that puts neighborhood proposals on a map residents can actually use.
- **Corey Samuel** — built the AHPT Dashboard, a staff-facing tool for tracking affordable housing property compliance.
- **Jayakanthan Narayan** — built 311RVA with the Transformers team, a resident-facing service request interface.
- **Marcus Thoreson** — built RVA Neighborhood Watch, a neighborhood activity monitoring tool.
- **Morgan Stuart** — built RVA Plotline (rva-plotline.fly.dev), a parcel and development tracking app.
- **Edwin Huertas** (CEO, Shockoe Inc) — built RVA Dev Tracker, a real-time development project dashboard.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Merrick Malone** — Director of Housing & Community Development, City of Richmond. Oversees CDBG, HOME, and the Affordable Housing Trust Fund — the funding streams most of these projects interact with.
- **Dr. Mae Worthey-Thomas** — CEO, Maggie Walker Community Land Trust. Leads Richmond's community land bank; appointed February 2025.
- **Zac Frederick** — Founder & CEO, Crescent Development. LIHTC affordable housing developer with deep knowledge of what compliance actually requires.
