Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Families Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two of Richmond's most important equity challenges: giving youth one clear, accessible path to summer jobs and first-career opportunities, and reducing the duplicated data work that fragments Richmond's maternal and family health ecosystem.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Toria Edmonds-Howell** — built Youth Employment RVA / Unboxed, this year's Families Pillar Award winner. A single entry point for Richmond youth to explore summer jobs, internships, and first-job guidance.
- **Sarang Shibu** — built Richmond People Data (richmond-people-data.vercel.app), a community demographic and service data layer.
- **Subhash Krishnamoorthy** — built RVA Community Platform (rvacommunityplatform.org), a family and youth services aggregator.
- **Sravan Kumar Borra** — built Ecowarriors, focused on environmental and family health outcomes.
- **Nana Kofi Okae** — built a Families-pillar project submitted via demo video.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Eva Colen** — Director of the Office of Children and Families, City of Richmond. The architect of Richmond's Universal Preschool initiative and the city's primary point of accountability for youth and family programs.
- **Dearsley Vernon** — VP of Programs, Communities in Schools of Richmond. Runs school-based wraparound services for Richmond students — the on-the-ground delivery infrastructure most of these tools need to connect to.
- **Jenna Pagano** — Founder, Pagano Wellness Clinic. Mental health and wellness practice; brings critical expertise on HIPAA compliance and Medicaid systems that any data-sharing project in this space must navigate.
