Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Economy Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two barriers that hold Richmond entrepreneurs back every day: minority-owned businesses struggling to find and compete for City contracts, and first-time entrepreneurs with no clear map of what it takes to start and register a business in Richmond.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Michael Mitchell & Rob Newton** (HopDrive) — built TradePath RVA (tradepathrva.com), this year's Economy Pillar Award winner. A discovery tool that matches minority-owned businesses with City contracting opportunities that fit their capabilities.
- **David Vilarino** — built iBuild (ibf-internal.red), a business formation navigation tool.
- **Jake Yesbeck** — built Career Bridge (river-city-hackers.onrender.com), connecting Richmond job seekers with local economic pathways.
- **Sam Pritchard** — built You Get What You Give (getwhatyougive-rva.com), a reciprocal skills and services exchange.
- **Mia Wang** — built hack4rva, a small business navigation tool.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Pat Foster** — Director of the Office of Minority Business Development, City of Richmond. Controls City procurement and MBE certification — the exact data that TradePath RVA needs to function at scale.
- **Floyd E. Miller II** — President & CEO, Metropolitan Business League. Leads a 50+ year organization serving minority-owned businesses across the Richmond region.
- **Melody Joy Short** — Founder, Short Consulting and Richmond Night Market. Small business consultant with deep roots in Richmond's vendor and maker economy.
