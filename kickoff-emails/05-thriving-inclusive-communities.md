Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Inclusive Communities Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two of Richmond's most sensitive and important challenges: helping immigrant and refugee residents find trusted community services without creating accounts or sharing identifying information, and helping residents navigate housing, workforce, and reentry services without repeating their story at every door.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Michael Mitchell** — built TradePath RVA (tradepathrva.com), which won both the Economy and Inclusive Communities Pillar Awards. The dual recognition reflects how closely economic access and community inclusion are linked.
- **Charles Wilson** — part of the TradePath RVA / 804me team (confirm which project to attribute for this pillar).
- **Whitney Zentgraf** — built rvahelps.me, a community resource navigation tool that lets residents find services without creating an account or sharing personal information.
- **CircleTrust Team** — built CircleTrust (github.com/circletrust/circletrust), a trust-network model for community service referrals.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Karla Almendarez-Ramos** — Manager of the Office of Immigrant & Refugee Engagement, City of Richmond. The person who runs daily translation and interpreter operations for the City — any tool in this space succeeds or fails based on whether she can use it.
- **Kate Ayers** — Executive Director, ReEstablish Richmond. Leads refugee resettlement and integration on the ground. (Note: Kate was traveling during the happy hour but is a core partner.)
- **Dr. Tiffany Jana** — Founder & CEO, TMI Consulting. Leads the world's first DEI-focused B Corp; brings equity measurement expertise and a critical eye for unintended exclusion in product design.
