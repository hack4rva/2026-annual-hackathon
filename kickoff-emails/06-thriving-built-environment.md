Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Built Environment Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two of Richmond's most visible infrastructure challenges: helping residents find and understand transportation and infrastructure projects happening in their neighborhoods, and improving how DPW supervisors and residents track the progress of services like snow removal and street cleaning in real time.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Jared Jarreau, Matthew Dinkel & Daniel Larner** — built Ride Shift RVA, this year's Moonshot Award winner. A rethinking of how Richmond tracks and communicates street-level service operations. The judges chose this as the project with the most transformative long-term potential.
- **Angelika Aquiro** (Team Aurea) — built StreetLights (ang-aq.github.io/Streetlights), a resident-facing infrastructure visibility tool.
- **William Baxter** — built All Your Bass, a service and infrastructure tracking interface.
- **Sasha Lewis** (Nova Foundry) — built a Built Environment project for the pillar; Nova Foundry is a local design and tech studio.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Dawn Oleksy** — Energy Program Manager, Office of Sustainability, City of Richmond. Leads RVAgreen 2050 and solar programs; managing a $250K clean energy grant. The City-side owner of sustainability infrastructure.
- **Rob Jones** — Executive Director, Groundwork RVA. Runs environmental justice work across Richmond including youth Green Teams — the community infrastructure layer these projects need to reach.
- **Patrick Farley** — Principal, Patrick Farley Architect. Net-zero design practitioner; delivered Richmond's first residential grid-tied solar PV installation. Brings the private-sector perspective on sustainable building.
