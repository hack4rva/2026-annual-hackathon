Hi everyone,

RVA Hacks is over — but the work is just beginning.

This is your **Storytelling Implementation Group**. You've been brought together around two of Richmond's most distinctly human challenges: giving residents a single discoverable place to find arts and cultural events happening in the city, and creating a trusted, accessible way for community members to share their lived experiences so that those stories can actually inform City decisions.

**Who's in this group:**

*Builders — the teams who built for this pillar:*
- **Daniel Blackin & Andrey V. Karpov** — built Neighborhood Remembered (richmondstories.online), this year's Storytelling Pillar Award winner. A platform for residents to record and share neighborhood histories and stories.
- **Tejesh Annavarapu** — built What's on RVA (whats-on-rva2.vercel.app), an aggregated Richmond arts and cultural events discovery tool.
- **Subhash Jaini** (Precizion) — built ExplorerVA (explorerva.org), a Richmond cultural discovery and exploration platform.
- **Kshiteej Herode** — built Vicino (vicino2.vercel.app), a neighborhood-proximity community connection tool.

*Steering Committee — domain leaders guiding implementation:*
- **Dr. Sylvio Lynch III** — Secretary of the History & Culture Commission and staff in Planning & Development Review, City of Richmond. The City's liaison for historical markers and monuments — and the person who was at the happy hour and knows exactly what's possible inside city government.
- **Enjoli Moon** — Founder, Afrikana Film Festival and The JXN Project. One of Richmond's most prominent cultural voices; leading the charge on Jackson Ward preservation. Any storytelling platform built here will need her trust.
- **Hannah Ayers** — Director and Co-Owner, Field Studio. Emmy-winning documentary filmmaker; produced PBS's "Future of America's Past." Brings the professional storytelling craft perspective to what these tools could become.
