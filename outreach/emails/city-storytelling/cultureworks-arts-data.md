I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, teams built prototypes for the "City That Tells Its Stories" pillar, specifically the Arts & Cultural Event Discovery problem statement: aggregating Richmond's arts and cultural events into one discoverable place.

Teams produced working prototypes around arts discovery, equity, and multilingual access.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/city-storytelling/cultureworks-arts-data.md)**

- CultureWorks runs on Localist with ICS, RSS, and JSON API access; it showcased **434 unique events** during artoberVA 2023 — the region's most comprehensive structured arts dataset.
- A live **Liquid syntax error** on the CultureWorks calendar blocks JSON-LD / schema.org Event data, hurting Google discoverability for every organization on the platform.
- Major institutions (VMFA, ICA, Richmond Ballet, Virginia Rep, Modlin) have **no open calendar feeds** — aggregators must scrape or negotiate per venue.
- ZIP codes **23222, 23223, and 23224** have materially lower broadband subscription rates; a web-only tool risks excluding those neighborhoods.

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
