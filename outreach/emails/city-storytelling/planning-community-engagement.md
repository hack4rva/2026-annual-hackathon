## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, teams built prototypes for the "City That Tells Its Stories" pillar, specifically the Resident Stories as Civic Insight problem statement: giving residents a trusted, accessible way to share lived experiences so community narratives can inform City decisions.

Teams produced working prototypes that connect resident voices to planning and policy.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/city-storytelling/planning-community-engagement.md)**

- The Diamond District process collected **1,300 survey responses**, four meetings, and **150 comments** — with **no published evidence** of how qualitative input shaped the final plan. *(Source: City of Richmond Diamond District planning process public records and published engagement summary, reviewed April 2026)*
- **~59% of Richmond households rent**, but zoning notices go to **property owners**, so renters are structurally easy to miss in the notification system. *(Source: U.S. Census Bureau ACS 2019–2023 5-year estimates; Richmond City Code zoning notice requirements)*
- Planning staff spend roughly **60% of time on application review** and **5% on community communication** — engagement functions as compliance, not a resourced program. *(Source: RVA Hacks Pillar 7 Working Session notes, March 2, 2026 — staff time estimates provided by working session attendees)*
- Santa Monica tied **qualitative resident wellbeing data to departmental workplans and budgets**; no US city we found runs a public system tracking how specific comments changed specific decisions. *(Source: Santa Monica Wellbeing Index program documentation; post-event comparative research, April 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
