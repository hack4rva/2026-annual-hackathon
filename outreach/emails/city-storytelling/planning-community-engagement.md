**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/city-storytelling/planning-community-engagement.md)**

- The Diamond District process collected **1,300 survey responses**, four meetings, and **150 comments** — with **no published evidence** of how qualitative input shaped the final plan.
- **~59% of Richmond households rent**, but zoning notices go to **property owners**, so renters are structurally easy to miss in the notification system.
- Planning staff spend roughly **60% of time on application review** and **5% on community communication** — engagement functions as compliance, not a resourced program.
- Santa Monica tied **qualitative resident wellbeing data to departmental workplans and budgets**; no US city we found runs a public system tracking how specific comments changed specific decisions.

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
