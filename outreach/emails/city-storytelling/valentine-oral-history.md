## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, teams built prototypes for the "City That Tells Its Stories" pillar, specifically the Resident Stories as Civic Insight problem statement: giving residents a trusted, accessible way to share lived experiences so community narratives can inform City decisions.

Teams produced working prototypes around neighborhood memory, archives, and consent-aware collection.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/city-storytelling/valentine-oral-history.md)**

- The **Historic Fulton Oral History Project** (2011–2012): **17 interviews, 32 participants**, Valentine + VCU + community partners; **The Valentine holds copyright**; non-commercial use allowed — **embedding in third-party tools is undocumented**. *(Source: The Valentine Museum collection documentation; VCU Scholars Compass digital archive metadata, reviewed April 2026)*
- **No public documentation** that VCU Scholars Compass / Islandora exposes a **stable IIIF or OAI-PMH API** for programmatic collection access. *(Source: Direct inspection of VCU Scholars Compass public documentation and available API endpoints, April 2026)*
- Digitized neighborhood oral histories also exist for **Jackson Ward, Church Hill, Carver, and Oregon Hill** — Richmond's recorded memory is rich but institutionally fragmented. *(Source: The Valentine Museum collection records; Library of Virginia digital collections catalog, reviewed April 2026)*
- **COPPA** requires verifiable parental consent before public posting of minors' photos/audio/video; a checkbox alone is not sufficient. *(Source: FTC COPPA rule, 16 CFR Part 312; FTC guidance on verifiable parental consent)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
