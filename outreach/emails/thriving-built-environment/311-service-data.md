## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Fleet tracking and infrastructure visibility tools — questions about 311 data and integration

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm [your name] from the RVA Hacks organizing team. We ran RVA Hacks, Richmond's March 2026 civic hackathon aligned to the Mayor's Action Plan. This note is about the **Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment** pillar — especially how 311 data and integration support **Infrastructure Project Visibility** and **Fleet Operations & Street Service Tracking**.

Several teams built prototypes that need 311 as a data signal, as a resident reporting channel, or both.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have five specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-built-environment/311-service-data.md)**

- Two teams built DPW fleet trackers for snow removal and street cleaning; another built a neighborhood infrastructure project map. *(Source: RVA Hacks 2026 demo day records, March 29, 2026 — Stay Informed, Road Maintenance Fleet Tracker, StreetLights)*
- Several tools either need 311 data as a signal or need to integrate with 311 so residents are not forced onto a separate reporting system. *(Source: RVA Hacks 2026 post-event prototype review; AvePoint Citizen Services platform — no public API currently documented)*
- Without real-time GPS, 311 service request patterns could still help surface which blocks see repeated missed service. *(Source: Post-event research, April 2026; public Socrata 311 dataset at data.richmondgov.com/resource/vgg4-hjn8 is SeeClickFix data 2013–2015 only — no current AvePoint data is public)*
- Built Environment demos (and projects in other pillars) lean on 311 for credible backends or routing — e.g. resident gap reporting tied to official intake. *(Source: RVA Hacks 2026 demo day cross-pillar analysis, April 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
