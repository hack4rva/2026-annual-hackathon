Hi [First Name],

I'm [your name] from the RVA Hacks organizing team. We ran RVA Hacks in March 2026 around the Mayor's Action Plan. This is about the **Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment** pillar — **Infrastructure Project Visibility**.

A team of three J.R. Tuggle High School juniors shipped **StreetLights**: a plain-language, resident-facing infrastructure map that pulls live data from Richmond GeoHub (https://ang-aq.github.io/Streetlights/).

We've completed extensive post-event research and have six specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-built-environment/gis-geohub-data.md)**

- The prototype uses real GeoHub feeds — unlike fleet demos that relied on synthetic data. *(Source: Direct inspection of StreetLights source code at ang-aq.github.io/Streetlights — confirmed live queries to cor.maps.arcgis.com REST API, April 2026)*
- The idea is to make official project data legible to ordinary residents; value scales directly with currency and accuracy of GeoHub records. *(Source: Post-event prototype review; CIP Dashboard dataset (125 records) provided by Chad Phillips, DPW GIS Team, per Pillar 6 Working Session notes, February 26, 2026)*
- If records lag or drift, a public tool risks misleading residents — so governance and update cadence are the open questions. *(Source: Direct inspection of GeoHub Plans of Development layer at cor.maps.arcgis.com — layer metadata shows last update June 29, 2022, nearly four years stale as of April 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
