## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Three service navigation prototypes — a few specific questions about RVA311 integration

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29, 2026 event, teams built service navigation prototypes for the **Thriving City Hall** pillar—Text 311, Hey804, and RVA Help—each aimed at helping residents find the right City service so requests route correctly the first time.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-city-hall/311-operations-avepoint-api.md)**

- **208,216** service requests in 2025 (up from ~83,000 in 2024); **116,000+** phone concerns resolved in 2025 without formal tickets—more than half of issues never become digital tickets. *(Source: RVA311 / Citizen Service & Response 2025 performance statistics, as reported by Pete Briel at the Pillar 1 Working Session, March 13, 2026)*
- The public **Socrata 311 dataset is legacy SeeClickFix (2013–2015)** only; **no current AvePoint-era data** is on the Open Data Portal. *(Source: data.richmondgov.com/resource/vgg4-hjn8 — direct inspection, April 2026; dataset metadata confirms last update 2015; AvePoint replaced SeeClickFix on June 15, 2018)*
- **AvePoint offers a Graph API** and BizTalk ties to Cityworks, but **no public RVA311 API** is documented—integration feasibility is the main unknown for prototypes. *(Source: AvePoint product documentation; Microsoft Azure BizTalk documentation; no public RVA311 API found in Richmond DIT published resources, April 2026)*
- **No comparable city** in our research has a verified AI chatbot or wizard-style navigation tool with documented outcomes—Richmond could be first if a prototype deployed. *(Source: Post-event comparative research, April 2026 — surveyed Norfolk, Raleigh, Charlotte, Alexandria, Arlington, and Virginia Beach)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
