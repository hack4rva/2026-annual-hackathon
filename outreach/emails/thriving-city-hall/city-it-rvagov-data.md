## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Service navigation research findings — and a specific data request for rva.gov

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. Our March 27–29, 2026 hackathon produced multiple **Thriving City Hall** service navigation prototypes; every team hit the same gap—**rva.gov** does not expose a machine-readable map of services, departments, and contacts.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **four** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-city-hall/city-it-rvagov-data.md)**

- **208,216** RVA311 requests in 2025, but **13.8% of households lack broadband** and **more than half** of concerns are still handled by phone—digital self-service is not reaching large segments of residents. *(Source: RVA311 2025 performance statistics per Pete Briel, Pillar 1 Working Session, March 13, 2026; FCC Broadband Data Collection and ACS 2019–2023 5-year estimates for Richmond City)*
- **No JSON-LD, sitemap.xml, or structured markup** for services; **rva.gov/common/services** is Drupal pages by department, not a task-oriented or API-consumable catalog. *(Source: Direct inspection of rva.gov HTML source, sitemap.xml, and page structure, April 2026)*
- **Language Access Policy** requires professional translation, but **71.57% of Richmond's LEP population speaks Spanish** and there is **no human-verified Spanish** RVA311 or rva.gov interface—the site's prominent Spanish content is often the disclaimer to call instead. *(Source: U.S. Census Bureau ACS 2019–2023 5-year estimates; City of Richmond Language Access Policy; direct inspection of rva.gov Spanish-language content, April 2026)*
- **Norfolk, Raleigh, Charlotte** have moved further on portals, chat, or knowledge bases; **no comparable city** has a verified AI chatbot or wizard-style nav—Richmond would be first if a prototype shipped. *(Source: Post-event comparative research, April 2026 — reviewed Norfolk 311, Raleigh 311, Charlotte CLT+, and state-level Virginia 211)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.

Thank you,
