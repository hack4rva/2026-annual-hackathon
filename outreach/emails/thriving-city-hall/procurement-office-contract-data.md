## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Four procurement prototypes — research findings and a few questions only your team can answer

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 2026 event, four teams built procurement review tools for the **Thriving City Hall** pillar—CivicPulse AI, Mira, Vendor Contract Mgmt, and RVA Contract Lens—each aimed at helping staff find compliant contracts across City, state, and federal sources without manual PDF hunting.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-city-hall/procurement-office-contract-data.md)**

- **Oracle RAPIDS + OnBase + OpenGov + iSupplier** form the live stack; **City Contracts** on Socrata (`xqn7-jvv2`, 1,367 rows, monthly updates) is the main dataset teams used—yet **735 distinct supplier strings** fragment what are likely far fewer real vendors. *(Source: data.richmondgov.com/resource/xqn7-jvv2 — direct inspection, April 2026; system stack per Pillar 1 Working Session notes, March 13, 2026)*
- **Average RFP cycle: 307 days** (FY2023) vs. **150-day target**; **32 FTE** in Procurement (FY2025)—capacity and cycle time are visible pain points in public reporting. *(Source: City of Richmond FY2025 Approved Budget, Procurement Services section; Procurement annual performance reporting)*
- **City MBE spend ~10% historically** with a **~9% FY2025 target** vs. **Virginia SWaM goal 42% (HB61, 2026)**—a large policy/measurement gap our tools might help clarify. *(Source: City of Richmond FY2025 budget documents; OMBD annual reporting; Virginia HB61 (2026 General Assembly session))*
- **P-card reset (April 2025):** 320 → **67** active cards after control reviews; **IG and City Auditor** findings on MBE and internal controls make **compliance tooling** (e.g., SAM.gov checks, spend matching) especially timely. *(Source: Richmond City Auditor special report on procurement internal controls, FY2025; Office of Inspector General findings on MBE compliance)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
