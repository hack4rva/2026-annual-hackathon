## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Business startup navigation prototypes — a few specific questions about BPOL and zoning data

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. RVA Hacks is Richmond's civic technology hackathon (March 27–29, 2026), organized around the Mayor's Action Plan; teams in the Thriving Economy pillar built prototypes to help first-time entrepreneurs understand how to start and register a business in Richmond.

Those prototypes focused on sequencing, costs, and what permits and licenses apply by business type.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have 5 specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-economy/finance-zoning-bpol.md)**

- **Mandatory registration sequence:** CZC is a hard prerequisite for BPOL, but most entrepreneurs discover that dependency only mid-application — it is not prominently documented in startup guides. *(Source: Pillar 4 Working Session notes, February 23, 2026; Richmond City Code § 17-102 (business license requirements); confirmed by direct tracing of rva.gov/finance application flow, April 2026)*
- **No business-type-to-permits mapping:** No single canonical, machine-readable resource maps Richmond business types to their full set of required permits, licenses, and tax obligations. *(Source: Direct inspection of rva.gov/finance, rva.gov/planning-development-review, and energov.richmondgov.com, April 2026)*
- **Checklist is PDF-only:** The City's Business License Application Checklist exists solely as a static PDF — no structured, machine-readable version was found on any public city channel. *(Source: Direct inspection of rva.gov/finance and City of Richmond document library, confirmed April 2026)*
- **No Virginia wizard:** No Virginia city or the state has deployed a personalized step-by-step business startup wizard comparable to NYC's MyCity Business or Chicago's Business Direct. *(Source: Post-event comparative research, April 2026 — reviewed Virginia Business One Stop (business.virginia.gov), Richmond, Norfolk, Roanoke, and Charlottesville startup guides)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
