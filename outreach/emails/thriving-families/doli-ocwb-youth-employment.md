## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. We ran RVA Hacks March 27–29, 2026, a civic technology weekend focused on Richmond's Mayor's Action Plan. Several teams built prototypes for the Thriving Families pillar on Youth Employment Pathways — one clear place for Richmond youth to explore summer jobs, internships, and first-job guidance.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-families/doli-ocwb-youth-employment.md)**

- **VAeECS is a closed system:** No APIs, deep-linking, or pre-population — each party must use the DOLI portal independently; external tools can only point users there. *(Source: Direct inspection of selfserviceportal.doli.virginia.gov and DOLI developer documentation, April 2026)*
- **Fragmented ecosystem:** Richmond runs 7+ parallel youth employment programs with different ages, requirements, and windows — and no shared referral backbone. *(Source: Pillar 3 Working Session notes, March 3, 2026; OCWB/Youth Engagement Services program inventory at rva.gov/community-wealth-building/youth-engagement-services)*
- **Foster youth barrier:** Virginia code provides no alternative signer for unaccompanied minors on work permits; guardianship must be established first. *(Source: Virginia Code § 40.1-79 (employment certificates for minors); DOLI child labor compliance guidance)*
- **Enforcement disparity:** 2018 RPD data (reported by WVTF) showed 98% of juveniles stopped for curfew violations were Black — relevant to late-shift work and youth employment. *(Source: WVTF Public Radio reporting on RPD curfew enforcement data, 2018 — relevant context for job hour access barriers)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
