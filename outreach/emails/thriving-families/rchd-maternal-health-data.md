## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. We ran RVA Hacks March 27–29, 2026, a civic technology weekend aligned with Richmond's Mayor's Action Plan. A team built a prototype for the Thriving Families pillar on Maternal Health Data Coordination — reducing duplicated analysis so Richmond's maternal health ecosystem can use shared, consistent metrics.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **five** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-families/rchd-maternal-health-data.md)**

- **PRAMS oversample:** RCHD is one of only two Virginia districts oversampled for PRAMS; in 2022, 348 of 977 statewide PRAMS respondents were RCHD residents — a premium local data asset. *(Source: Virginia PRAMS (Pregnancy Risk Assessment Monitoring System) annual report, Virginia Department of Health; VDH PRAMS methodology documentation)*
- **Six-portal fragmentation:** Maternal health data is spread across at least six portals with different time windows, suppression rules, and definitions — a root cause of conflicting numbers across organizations. *(Source: Post-event research mapping across VDH, CDC WONDER, Census ACS, DMAS, Kids Count, and Virginia BRFSS portals, April 2026; Pillar 3 Working Session notes, March 3, 2026)*
- **City MMR gap:** VDH does not publish Richmond City maternal mortality ratio due to suppression; statewide, Non-Hispanic Black MMR is 69.9 vs. 13.2 per 100k live births for Non-Hispanic White — a 5.3× disparity. *(Source: Virginia Department of Health maternal mortality surveillance report; VDH data suppression policy for small-count localities)*
- **No unified U.S. city model:** No U.S. city yet consolidates VDH, Census, and CDC maternal data into one race-disaggregated view like the hackathon prototype attempted. *(Source: Post-event comparative research, April 2026 — reviewed maternal health data dashboards in Baltimore, Detroit, Memphis, and Chicago)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
