## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Cross-agency navigation prototypes for reentry residents — specific questions about intake workflows, consent forms, and Unite Us adoption

## Body

Hi [First Name(s)],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29, 2026 event, teams built prototypes for the "Thriving and Inclusive Communities" pillar, addressing cross-agency navigation for housing, workforce, and reentry services.

Teams explored resident-facing tools so people do not have to repeat their full story at every agency.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have five specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-inclusive-communities/dss-reentry-services.md)**

- **Five-system fragmentation:** Richmond's social safety net runs on five legacy systems that share zero client identifiers — the same resident can appear as three unrelated new clients across DSS, workforce, and housing pathways. *(Source: Pillar 5 Working Session notes, March 6, 2026 — confirmed by Kate Ayers and Helen Rai (ReEstablish Richmond) and NCS staff)*
- **Case manager burden:** Case managers at Richmond nonprofits spend an estimated 6–10 hours per week on coordination, referral tracking, and directory searching — Help1RVA and Unite Us do not natively exchange data. *(Source: Pillar 5 Working Session notes, March 6, 2026 — staff time estimates from nonprofit attendees; Unite Us and findhelp platform documentation confirming no native interoperability)*
- **Reentry 72-hour window:** Supervised individuals must report within 72 hours of release; RCJC organizes support around six pillars, but no single public source quantifies how many intake forms a returning resident completes in the first week. *(Source: Virginia Department of Corrections supervised release requirements; Richmond City Justice Center (RCJC) public documentation; post-event research, April 2026)*
- **Institutional friction:** City MOUs average an estimated 128 days; DOTI requires seven signatures and two security scans for third-party data access — a shared cross-agency data system is not a near-term assumption. *(Source: Post-event research, April 2026 — Richmond DIT/DOTI third-party data access process documentation; MOU timeline estimate from Pillar 5 Working Session notes, March 6, 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
