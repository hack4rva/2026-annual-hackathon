## Subject
RVA Hacks 2026: Immigrant service discovery prototypes — specific questions about OIRE directory data, CBO co-branding, and navigator capacity

## Body

Hi [First Name(s)],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29, 2026 event, teams built prototypes for the "Thriving and Inclusive Communities" pillar, addressing safe discovery of trusted community services without accounts or unnecessary identifying information.

Teams focused on channel-based and navigator-mediated tools that respect how residents already seek help.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have five specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-inclusive-communities/oire-immigrant-services.md)**

- **Help1RVA at scale:** Help1RVA lists 1,800+ programs on findhelp, but there is no free public API — programmatic access is limited to paying partners. *(Source: findhelp.org platform documentation and partner terms; direct inspection of Help1RVA.org, April 2026)*
- **Trust and chilling effects:** A 2022 VCU/ONA study documented a significant trust deficit between immigrant communities and government; recent courthouse ICE activity in the region has intensified fear of initiating contact. *(Source: VCU / Outlier News Alliance (ONA) 2022 immigrant community trust study; Pillar 5 Working Session notes, March 6, 2026 — confirmed by Kate Ayers and Helen Rai)*
- **Channels OIRE confirmed:** Facebook groups, WhatsApp, Instagram, YouTube, and local radio are the primary ways many Richmond immigrant communities get information — not government websites alone. *(Source: Pillar 5 Working Session notes, March 6, 2026 — confirmed by Karla Almendarez-Ramos (NCS), OIRE staff, and ReEstablish Richmond)*
- **No sustained anonymous directory:** USAHello discontinued FindHello as the responsible path forward; no US city has kept a sustained, anonymous, immigrant-specific directory past pilot stage without strong platform partnerships and governance. *(Source: USAHello public announcement on FindHello discontinuation; post-event comparative research across immigrant service directories in 8 US cities, April 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
