Hi [First Name(s)],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29, 2026 event, teams built prototypes for the "Thriving and Inclusive Communities" pillar, addressing safe discovery of trusted community services without accounts or unnecessary identifying information.

Teams focused on channel-based and navigator-mediated tools that respect how residents already seek help.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have five specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-inclusive-communities/oire-immigrant-services.md)**

- **Help1RVA at scale:** Help1RVA lists 1,800+ programs on findhelp, but there is no free public API — programmatic access is limited to paying partners.
- **Trust and chilling effects:** A 2022 VCU/ONA study documented a significant trust deficit between immigrant communities and government; recent courthouse ICE activity in the region has intensified fear of initiating contact.
- **Channels OIRE confirmed:** Facebook groups, WhatsApp, Instagram, YouTube, and local radio are the primary ways many Richmond immigrant communities get information — not government websites alone.
- **No sustained anonymous directory:** USAHello discontinued FindHello as the responsible path forward; no US city has kept a sustained, anonymous, immigrant-specific directory past pilot stage without strong platform partnerships and governance.

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
