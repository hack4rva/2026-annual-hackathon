## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, four teams built tools for the "Thriving Neighborhoods" pillar — three focused on development discovery near residents and one on affordable housing compliance for City staff.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **3** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-neighborhoods/assessor-data-integration.md)**

- The Assessor's parcel data is available via the Richmond GeoHub ArcGIS REST API and a free monthly Excel bulk export — a strong public foundation for mapping and joins. *(Source: cor.maps.arcgis.com — direct inspection of parcel data layer; Richmond City Assessor's Office website, confirmed April 2026)*
- Parcel IDs are the best candidate for cross-system matching, but EnerGov has no public API and Legistar buries parcel IDs in PDFs, so programmatic linkage to those systems is unconfirmed. *(Source: EnerGov / Tyler Technologies product documentation; Legistar OData API documentation (webapi.legistar.com) — direct inspection confirmed no structured parcel ID field in meeting records, April 2026)*
- A documented 7-step AHTEP handoff runs between HCD and the Assessor; it isn't clear whether a shared system tracks Base Value Reports or whether coordination is mainly email-based. *(Source: Pillar 2 Working Session notes, February 18, 2026 — Rachel Hightman (HCD) described the AHTEP process; AHPG dataset provided by Rachel Hightman)*
- A February 2026 City Auditor special review flagged gaps in affordable housing compliance infrastructure and recommended formalizing data sources — property-level integration matters for any follow-on tooling. *(Source: Richmond City Auditor Special Report on the Affordable Housing Trust Fund, February 2026 — public document)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
