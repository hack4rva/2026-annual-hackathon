## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, one team built an Affordable Housing Compliance Dashboard for the "Thriving Neighborhoods" pillar, aimed at helping City staff track whether publicly funded affordable housing stays affordable over time.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **5** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-neighborhoods/housing-community-development.md)**

- The February 2026 OCA special review found systemic AHTF implementation gaps and **$2,472,307** untransferred since FY2022; the administration concurred with all eight high-priority recommendations. *(Source: Richmond City Auditor (OCA) Special Report on the Affordable Housing Trust Fund, February 2026 — public document)*
- AHTF alone funded **822** units in FY2024 and **580** in FY2025, each with 15-year monitoring obligations — yet there is **no public** consolidated registry or GIS layer flagging subsidized affordable housing. *(Source: AHPG dataset and HCD Projects dataset (198 records) provided by Rachel Hightman (HCD) at Pillar 2 Working Session, February 18, 2026; post-event GeoHub inspection confirmed no public compliance layer, April 2026)*
- EnerGov has no public API; whether it stores structured AHTEP fields (units by AMI, rents) vs. mostly status and fees will determine if a dashboard can plug in or needs a parallel data layer. *(Source: EnerGov / Tyler Technologies product documentation; Richmond EnerGov instance at energov.richmondgov.com — confirmed no public API, April 2026)*
- Denver, San Francisco, Chicago, LA, and DC all run more mature compliance tracking than Richmond today — your answers help us ground what’s realistic locally. *(Source: Post-event comparative research, April 2026 — reviewed Denver Housing Authority compliance portal, SF Mayor’s Office of Housing DAHLIA, Chicago IHDA compliance system, and DC DHCD affordable housing map)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
