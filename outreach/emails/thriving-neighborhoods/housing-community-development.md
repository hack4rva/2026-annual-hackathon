- The February 2026 OCA special review found systemic AHTF implementation gaps and **$2,472,307** untransferred since FY2022; the administration concurred with all eight high-priority recommendations.
- AHTF alone funded **822** units in FY2024 and **580** in FY2025, each with 15-year monitoring obligations — yet there is **no public** consolidated registry or GIS layer flagging subsidized affordable housing.
- EnerGov has no public API; whether it stores structured AHTEP fields (units by AMI, rents) vs. mostly status and fees will determine if a dashboard can plug in or needs a parallel data layer.
- Denver, San Francisco, Chicago, LA, and DC all run more mature compliance tracking than Richmond today — your answers help us ground what’s realistic locally.

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
