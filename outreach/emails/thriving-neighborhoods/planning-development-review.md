RVA Hacks 2026: Three development discovery prototypes — research findings and a few questions only your department can answer

## Body

Hi [First Name],

I'm writing from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon team. During our March 27–29 event, three teams built neighborhood development discovery tools for the "Thriving Neighborhoods" pillar: RVA Plotlines, RVA Development Tracker, and SIMBY.

We've completed extensive post-event research and have **5** specific questions for your team. We've documented our confirmed findings and detailed questions here:

**[View Full Research & Questions](https://github.com/hack4rva/2026-annual-hackathon/blob/main/outreach/questions/thriving-neighborhoods/planning-development-review.md)**

- Richmond's Legistar OData API is publicly accessible (no token); cross-linking Legistar to ArcGIS is a proven national pattern — the technical path for legislative-to-map tools is real. *(Source: Legistar OData API documentation at webapi.legistar.com — confirmed publicly accessible, no auth required, April 2026; national comparator research showing similar integrations in Boston, Chicago, and Seattle)*
- The GeoHub **Plans of Development** REST layer exists but was last updated **June 29, 2022** — nearly four years stale — while EnerGov has **no** public API or documented export, the largest gap for permit-linked discovery. *(Source: cor.maps.arcgis.com Plans of Development layer — direct inspection, layer metadata confirms June 29, 2022 last update; EnerGov product documentation confirms no public API)*
- Legal notice for rezonings and SUPs goes only to owners within 150 feet; **~59%** of city households are renters, so most residents are structurally excluded from direct mailed notice. *(Source: Richmond City Code § 30-792 (zoning notice requirements); U.S. Census Bureau ACS 2019–2023 5-year estimates, Richmond City renter/owner breakdown)*
- National civic tech research shows surviving post-hackathon tools share **stable APIs** and institutional stewardship; failures trace to data drift and no maintainers. *(Source: Code for America civic technology sustainability studies; ICMA research on post-hackathon tool longevity; post-event comparative research, April 2026)*

We're not asking for any commitment — just trying to understand what's technically possible so we can give the Mayor's office a realistic assessment. Happy to share any prototypes or our full research.
