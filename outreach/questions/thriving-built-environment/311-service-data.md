# Outreach: 311 / Customer Experience Operations Lead — Research & Questions

I'm [your name] from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon organizing team. At our March 2026 event, several teams built civic technology prototypes that would benefit from 311 data access or integration. Two teams built DPW fleet trackers for snow removal and street cleaning; another built a neighborhood infrastructure project map. Several of these tools either need 311 data as a signal, or need to integrate with 311 as a reporting channel for residents.

I'm reaching out with a few questions about what's possible — not to make a commitment or request, but to understand the landscape before we advise teams on what a realistic path to deployment looks like.

**Our questions:**
1. Is Richmond's 311 service request data available via an open API (e.g., through data.richmondgov.com)? If so, which service request categories are available, and what is the typical data freshness?
2. Are DPW-related service request categories — specifically snow removal complaints, missed street cleaning, pothole/infrastructure reports — broken out in the 311 data, or do they come in under generic categories?
3. Is there any equity data available on 311 call volume or service response times by neighborhood? (We've seen research from other cities showing systematic disparities — curious if Richmond has analyzed this internally.)
4. Would the City consider an API or data-sharing arrangement with a civic tool that routes resident issue reports directly to 311, rather than requiring residents to use a separate system?
5. Does Richmond currently have a plan for a 311 mobile app or enhanced notification system? We want to make sure any prototypes we advise teams on are compliant with or complementary to City plans.

None of these questions require a commitment. We're trying to give the teams realistic guidance about what real 311 integration would require.
