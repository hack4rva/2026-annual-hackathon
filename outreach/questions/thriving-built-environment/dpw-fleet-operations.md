# Outreach: DPW Fleet Operations Lead — Research & Questions

I'm [your name] from the RVA Hacks civic hackathon organizing team. At our March 2026 event, two separate teams built working prototypes of resident-facing and staff-facing DPW fleet tracking tools — one called "Stay Informed" and another called "Road Maintenance Fleet Tracker." Both built exactly the kind of dual-portal system you'd want: residents can check whether their street has been plowed, and supervisors get a real-time fleet dashboard.

There's just one problem: both teams had to use completely synthetic data because they didn't have access to real DPW fleet telemetry. The UIs are solid. The data layer is fiction.

We'd love to have a 20-minute conversation to understand what real data actually exists within DPW, so that if the City ever wanted to move these prototypes toward a real pilot, we know what the actual integration path looks like.

Would you be willing to answer a few questions by email, or jump on a brief call?

**Our questions:**
1. Does Richmond DPW currently have GPS or telematics devices on plow trucks and street sweepers? If so, what system (Samsara, Verizon Connect, other)?
2. If GPS data exists, does it currently export in any structured format (API, CSV, database)? Is that data accessible outside of DPW's internal systems?
3. How does DPW currently assign and track routes during a snow emergency? Is there a software system, or is it dispatch radio and spreadsheets?
4. Is Richmond's street cleaning schedule published anywhere in a structured format, or is it managed internally?
5. What would the right entry point be for a pilot — would this go through IT, procurement, or does DPW handle vendor integrations independently?

We're not asking for a commitment or anything proprietary. We just want to understand what's real so we can give these teams honest advice about what a path to deployment would actually require.
