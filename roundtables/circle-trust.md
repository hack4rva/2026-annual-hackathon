## Seven personas (one stance each)

1. **Pollinator / organizer** — Circle Trust is the right layer: 300+ verified change makers, Circles like Jackson Ward graffiti removal, and invitation-only access protect relational accountability and keep bad actors off coordination spaces. The philosophy (coordinated infrastructure) matches how real neighborhoods actually move work.

2. **Resident in PS2 shoes (reentry / housing / workforce)** — I never appeared in the demo. PS2 is about me not retelling my story at DSS, VCW, OAR, and housing providers. An invite-only app for “people in the work” is not my front door; I need low-barrier navigation, not pollinator credentials.

3. **Frontline case manager** — I might benefit if my peers were in Circles with clear referral norms, but nothing showed referral tracking, Unite Us/Help1RVA hooks, or “did the client arrive?” closure. Right now this is adjacent mobilization tech, not case coordination.

4. **City / DIT realist** — No AvePoint, Unite Us, or Help1RVA API path was claimed. That matches the tech stack doc: those integrations need partnership. A nonprofit-hosted coordination tool can ship without the City, but it also does not reduce agency intake duplication by itself.

5. **Privacy & equity advocate** — The research bundle (RefAid-style asymmetric vetting, anti–Nextdoor surveillance, linker-led onboarding) is directionally strong. Tension: invitation-only verification for helpers can be inclusive for immigrants *if* gatekeeping is fair—but it can also reproduce insider networks unless outreach to marginalized communities is explicit and funded.

6. **Mutual-aid technologist** — INNOVATION_ANALYSIS is right: the win is governance and resilience (multi-rail payments, deplatforming drills, role-based IAM), not a novel CRUD app. rubyforgood/mutual-aid and Signal-first ops are credible foundations; the hard part is adoption and abuse control on anonymous intake—if you ever add it.

7. **Judge / pillar champion** — Score the demo against the **stated** PS2 problem and you see a mismatch. Score it as **community coordination infrastructure** and you see traction, a live Circle, and a serious research-backed trust narrative. The team’s ask for more time is fair; judges still only have what was shown.

## Fatal flaw

**PS2 misalignment as demonstrated:** No resident-facing flow, no shared intake or referral tracking, no touch to Help1RVA/Unite Us/VaCMS/HCIS/VaWC. The “coordinated infrastructure” thesis is philosophically related but **operationally a different product** than cross-agency navigation. Until a concrete resident path and at least one measurable handoff outcome are shown, the project fails the problem statement it was bucketed under—while still potentially valuable under “community governance / mutual aid infrastructure.”

---

## Verdict

**Conditional nurture, relabel scope.** Do not score as a complete PS2 solution without new evidence. **Do** credit real network scale (300+ pollinators), honest time-boxing, and research-aligned trust design. Next proof points: (1) one PS2-relevant Circle with named agency partners, (2) pollinator→resident→provider journey on camera, (3) complementary positioning with anonymous service discovery if City-facing navigation stays out of scope.
