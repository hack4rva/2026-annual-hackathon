## Step 2 — Seven Persona Assessments

### THE INNOVATOR

The non-obvious version is not “another oral history site” but a **federation layer**: one discovery front door that preserves institutional custody while raising **policy salience** of narratives already held at VCU, the Library of Virginia, JXN Project, The Valentine, and UofR’s Digital Scholarship Lab. Cross-domain pattern: **HOPE-style federated archives** use a central access node plus OAI-PMH metadata harvesting so users discover union-catalog-style results without migrating master files—an established humanities infrastructure move applied here to displacement memory. The ERRC grid is directionally right (eliminate silos, raise spatial + descendant authority, create policy pathway) but underweights **ongoing harvest** versus one-time StoryMap launches; research on Houston IMLS digitization and “roadsTaken”-style builds shows durability comes from braided staffing, not a single Esri artifact. Challenging the grid: “Create NEPA packaging” is bold; **St. Paul Branch of NAACP v. USDOT** confirms federal environmental review can engage historic highway harm (Rondo-style context in EIS), but tying *oral history clips* specifically to admissibility is less documented than “cumulative impact framing” generally—treat policy pathway as **design intent requiring legal/partner validation**, not a shipped guarantee.

**WebSearch:** St. Paul NAACP / USDOT NEPA EIS Rondo historical impacts; federated digital archive OAI-PMH oral history OHMS.

---

### THE ACTIVIST

**Decision tree: PARTNER + ADAPT (primary); light BUILD on the federation UX.**

Similar **mapped displacement narratives** exist nationally (Anti-Eviction Mapping Project, Preserving Society Hill, Houston roadsTaken, Pasadena SR-710 oral history report). Richmond’s gap is **integration and narrative power across institutions**, not the absence of testimony. The **City should not own narrator data or interpretation**; the right posture is municipal **signal of support** (e.g., alignment with Reconnect Jackson Ward communications, grants, public history partnerships) while **community-led and library/university custodians** retain collections. Alexandria’s **Colored Rosemont Community History Initiative** offers a verified Virginia precedent: descendant advisory group since 2022, oral histories in city oral history center, state highway marker dedication September 2025—exactly the governance pattern this idea proposes for Richmond. Scale: high for other mid-sized highway cities if the stack stays link-first and rights-audited.

**WebSearch:** Alexandria Colored Rosemont Descendant Advisory Group; Colored Rosemont state historic marker 2025.

---

### THE CITIZEN

**Engaged resident:** High resonance—before/after map toggles and pinned clips make abstract “urban renewal” tangible on the ground they walk. **Service user** (student, teacher, journalist): Strong if the portal surfaces citations, permissions, and stable URLs; weak if it is only a pretty map with broken deep links. **Invisible resident:** People without broadband, elders, and those not in “archive-ready” networks may never contribute or see the site unless paired with **exhibitions, listening events, school modules, and low-bandwidth paths** (pillar INDEX: B3 longtime resident, B5 digital equity). Who is left out: **renters and newer residents** in gentrifying blocks may feel spoken *about* not *with*; **non-English narrators** if the MVP is English-only; **families without legal clarity on releases** if collections mirror DC “Southwest Views” restriction scenarios.

---

### THE MAYOR

This can be defended at a podium as **truth-telling aligned with ongoing repair work**—Richmond’s **Reconnect Jackson Ward** program (city `.gov` planning pages, Reconnecting Communities pilot grant coverage) gives a concrete policy hook so the narrative is “memory informing reconnection,” not only retrospective guilt. Quick-win potential is **medium**: a weekend StoryMap chapter is demo-ready, but a press conference promise requires **partner buy-in and rights language** so the City is not blamed for overclaiming or misembedding someone’s voice. Political risk: opponents may frame it as anti-development storytelling; mitigation is **fact-grounded overlays** (Renewing Inequality, LoV exhibit scholarship) and **descendant-led framing**, not City-authored interpretation.

**WebSearch:** Reconnect Jackson Ward Richmond federal grant planning; rva.gov reconnect pages.

---

### THE ARCHITECT

**Simplest thing that works:** Static or lightly dynamic **curated index page** + one **ArcGIS StoryMap** chapter (Jackson Ward) embedding only **cleared** clips and links to source repositories—no wholesale migration. **What breaks first:** (1) **link rot** when partner URLs or platforms change; (2) **rights surprises** if embed terms differ from public reading terms; (3) **Esri licensing** if hosted outside an institution that already holds ArcGIS Online (nonprofit pricing exists but **someone** must own the subscription long-term). At **12 months**, without a named archivist or part-time digital curator, the StoryMap becomes stale and the “federation” devolves into an unmaintained bookmark list—maintenance burden is **organizational**, matching RESEARCH_RESULT’s braided funding thesis.

**WebSearch:** ArcGIS StoryMaps licensing nonprofit ArcGIS Online ~$100/yr tier (creator types still required for authoring).

---

### THE FRONTLINER

**Matches real work** for educators, planners, and advocates who already stitch together VCU Scholars Compass, LoV exhibits, and UofR DSL maps manually; a **single entry point** saves hours and reduces errors in citations. **Friction:** Frontline city staff rarely drive oral-history integration unless a **specific project** (cap study, EIS comment period, heritage commission item) demands it—so adoption spikes around **policy windows** and events, not daily dashboards. Target users **will use it** if outputs map to their artifacts: **downloadable fact sheets**, stable citations, and **explicit “what you may/may not reuse”** labels; they will skip it if it feels like performative tech without institutional phone numbers and contact paths for permissions.

---

### THE FUNDER

**Cost:** Weekend prototype ≈ volunteer time + possible Esri seat (or in-kind from a partner university) + minimal hosting for a portal page. **Serious program** resembles Houston Public Library’s IMLS-scale line items (digitization vendor, project archivist, supplies)—order of **tens to low hundreds of thousands** if expanding transcripts, OHMS, and preservation masters. **Year 2:** Not self-sustaining on goodwill; needs **braided** IMLS/NEH/Mellon-style grants plus **institutional FTE** (library or community nonprofit). **Who pays:** Universities, LoV, philanthropy, and competitive federal humanities/transport justice streams—not the City general fund alone.

**WebSearch:** IMLS museum grants sample budget oral history digitization (from research doc); Esri nonprofit program pricing summary.

## Step 3 — Top 3 Tensions and Resolutions

| # | Tension | Resolution |
|---|---------|------------|
| 1 | **City branding vs. community custody** — Mayor wants a civic win; activists resist City “owning” trauma narratives. | **Partner-led federation** with City as **connector/funder of public history**, not host of restricted assets; narrators remain under existing deed-of-gift chains. |
| 2 | **Policy-grade “evidence” vs. ethics/restrictions** — IDEA promises testimony for reviews; DC Southwest Views shows **no release = research-only**. | Ship only **rights-cleared embeds**; label uncertainty; add **legal/partner review gate** before any “NEPA kit” claims; default to education and advocacy briefs, not guaranteed docket admissibility. |
| 3 | **Federation without migration vs. user expectation of unified search** — True federated search needs OAI-PMH/API cooperation partners may not grant quickly. | **Phase 1:** curated chapter + themed link hub + consistent metadata template; **Phase 2:** harvest protocols (OAI-PMH / OHMS exports) once LOIs exist. |

## Step 4 — Fatal Flaw Test

| Test | Result | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Data dependency** | **Conditional pass** | Rich assets exist (VCU Fulton, LoV House to Highway, UofR Renewing Inequality); **failure mode** is permissions, not missing tapes. |
| **Structural wall** | **Pass** | Does not require 311, EnerGov, OnBase, B2GNow, or DPW GPS (per `RICHMOND_TECH_STACK_2026.md`). |
| **Ownership vacuum** | **Risk** | Without a **named consortium steward** (e.g., JXN + LoV + VCU Libraries charter), the portal stalls after demo. Mitigate with MOUs/LOIs before public launch. |
| **Harm potential** | **Conditional pass** | Fine-grained mapping can **chill or instrumentalize** place-based trauma; INNOVATION_ANALYSIS correctly flags AEMP-style location sensitivity—**descendant board + aggregation options** required. |
| **Political exposure** | **Moderate** | Honest history invites backlash; pairing with **Reconnect Jackson Ward** and descendant co-sign reduces “City propaganda” readings. |

**No single fatal flaw** if governance and rights gates are treated as **preconditions**, not post-hoc polish.

---

## Step 5 — Verdict

### SHIP WITH CONDITIONS

**Conditions (minimum):**

1. **Letters of intent** from at least three of: VCU Libraries, Library of Virginia, JXN Project (and clarity with The Valentine on Fulton rights/embed rules).  
2. **Rights audit** of every clip in the MVP; no “public website” assumption without deed-of-gift alignment.  
3. **Descendant / community advisory body** chartered **before** marketing as “Richmond’s” portal—modeled on verified Colored Rosemont practice.  
4. **Named maintenance owner** for link checks and StoryMap updates through month 12 (FTE fraction or formal volunteer agreement with institution).

## Step 7 — Narrative Summary

The Displacement Memory Archive is strongest when framed as **federated discovery + spatial narrative** across Richmond’s already-substantial institutional holdings, not as a new collection drive. Implementation avoids Richmond’s enterprise **structural walls** because it leans on universities, state library, and open scholarship outputs; the real risks are **governance, consent, and maintenance**, which are solvable with Alexandria-style descendant partnership and documented rights. **SHIP WITH CONDITIONS** captures that the idea is demo-ready and strategically timed to **Reconnect Jackson Ward**, but should not ship as a City-owned megaphone without partner MOUs and a rights-audited MVP.
